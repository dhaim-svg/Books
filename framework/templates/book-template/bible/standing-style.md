## POV and reveal contract

POV: Third-person limited, the protagonist only. A supporting character's interiority stays off-page;
we read them through what they do and say. This protects the shared-reveal contract.

**Reader and protagonist learn together.** The planted details, the central reversal, and the
culprit's identity are discovered at the same moment by both. The shared-reveal contract is
non-negotiable — the partner's interiority stays off-page even in scenes where their insight would
be dramatically satisfying.

## Tense

Past tense throughout.

## Tone

> _Set your book's tonal surface here (this template's example was a cozy beach-read with one death that carries real weight). Define what kind of weight the book carries and how it is carried._

Banter is welcome. Grim atmosphere is not (adjust for your genre). The weight is carried by how characters treat each other, not by darkness on the page.

## Game / System layer (if any)

> _If your book has a game/System layer:_ Use System screens sparsely — only when they earn the interruption. Format them as indented italic blocks, not as HUD-style boxes. The protagonist reacts to them in character, not with wonder and not with boredom. If your book has no game layer, ignore this section.

## Prose

Lean, image-led. Avoid stacked adjectives. No Capitalised Worldbuilding Nouns unless the bible
uses them that way. Dialogue carries character; don't overstuff tags.

## Plant discipline

When a planted detail lands, plant it — don't underline it. The reader passes it without
stopping. **Don't cluster plants in one scene.** Two plants in the same step are fine if they
are in different scenes; two plants in the same scene softens the surprise.

Only after the Act 2 reveal (Step 6) should the planted details come back, in the quiet recall
passage where the protagonist and partner sit together and the details rearrange into a person.
That is the only moment we name what happened.

> _List your book's planted details here — textural, not investigative._

## What to never do

- Do not summarize the bible into the chapter
- Do not foreshadow the Act 2 reveal directly
- Do not have any character "almost catch" the central reversal
- Do not explain the game/System layer to the reader (if your book has one)
- Do not give the reveal-protecting supporting character interiority — action and dialogue only
- Do not use HUD-style System boxes (if your book has a game layer)
- Do not Capitalise Worldbuilding Nouns not already in the bible
