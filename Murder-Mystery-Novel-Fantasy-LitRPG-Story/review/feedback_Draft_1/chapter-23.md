# Chapter 23 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — kept reading past page one without hesitation. The opening image (clerk's finger at rest, the smell of sealing wax, the slow rectangle of harbor light on flagstone) is specific and grounding, and the dynamic between Theron and the clerk earns a small, dry smile without trying too hard. The pace is deliberate but the prose earns it: each detail does something (texture, character, forward motion), and none of them overstay. No stopping point; the scene pulled me straight through into the walking scene that follows.

### 2. Planted details

One detail stood out: Joren in the corridor, reaching into his coat for something small and looking at it before putting it back. It felt natural — the moment is filtered through Esherel's secondhand account, which creates the right amount of remove. The detail does not feel planted in the sense of being italicised or underlined; it lands as a piece of testimony that matters to a side character for personal reasons. The carved fish is mentioned by name only after the detail is established, which is the correct order. Neither detail stopped me in a suspicious way. One smooth pass.

### 3. POV character

Theron works. He is precise without being cold, and the scene where he nearly calls the niece's information "generous" and catches himself — settling for "that would mean something" — does more character work in four words than a page of interiority would. The method-review section could read as naval-gazing, but it is saved by the absence of self-pity: he does not blame himself, he just reclassifies. That distinction reads clearly. One hundred thousand words with him: yes, with mild reservations only about the pacing in the middle section.

### 4. Tone calibration

Cozy-correct throughout. The chapter's quietness is earned, not flat: there is real weight in the Esherel-via-niece scene, but it never tips into grim. The method-review passage is the one place the tone goes slightly slow — three consecutive paragraphs of Theron thinking about thinking risk losing the reader who came for the harbor and the people. The scene recovers well once Esherel arrives. No moments of tonal wrongness; the risk is pacing-flatness in the middle third, not darkness or false comedy.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "A dead end, possibly. A Toll shell, possibly. Another layer requiring work in Halverstow before he could say anything useful." — the triple-possibly structure deflates rather than conveys. One clause does the work.

**Keep:** "She said he didn't look sad. He just looked like a man who knew what mattered." — clean, quiet, weight-bearing without ornament.
