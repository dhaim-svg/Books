# Chapter 13 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading. The cold open is clean and functional: a folded paper, a name, a corridor to walk. The mechanism of the chapter is visible from the first exchange (Sable has found something; now we follow it), and the gondola's physical texture — lamp brackets, the dragon's pull through the soles of your feet — is atmospheric without being decorative. No single line stopped me. The one soft patch is "This was, Theron reflected, the specific kind of information the System had no good mechanism for registering" — it's a valid observation but it sits slightly outside the chapter's momentum and could be cut without loss. Everything before and after it is tighter.

### 2. Planted details

Two details stood out. The first: the pale grey coat with the half-cape, "too fine for third class by two price brackets at minimum." Noticed, moved on — it passes as texture. The second: the carved wooden fish mentioned in the penultimate scene. This one stopped me briefly, because it arrives with a specific backstory note ("not in the luggage, which had been on the body") that signals the reader to remember it. It reads more pointed than the coat, closer to planted than textural. It's brief enough that it doesn't break anything, but the parenthetical logic ("had not been in the luggage, which had been on the body") frames it as a remembered clue rather than an ambient detail.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic and worth 100,000 words. Theron is precise without being cold, and his handling of Vannic — "It comes out more gently than he intended," the breakfast instruction — shows the gap between his professional manner and his actual character in a way that's endearing without being cloying. The apology exchange with Sable at the end is the chapter's best characterisation beat: he's wrong, she's right, and he knows it and deflects anyway, and the reader likes him more for it. No distancing.

### 4. Tone calibration

Cozy throughout, including the reveal. The identity-swap disclosure lands as surprise-and-embarrassment rather than horror, which is exactly right. The one moment that grazes the wrong register is the stairwell landing passage where Theron works through the targeted-killing logic ("Killing a man who is performing Vannic and knowing he is performing Vannic is targeted"). The logic is correct for the genre, but three iterations of the same sentence structure in rapid succession makes it feel more procedural-thriller than beach-read. One pass instead of three would keep the cozy register intact without losing the deduction.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "This was, Theron reflected, the specific kind of information the System had no good mechanism for registering — not evidence, not observation, just a steward's competent unease holding a paper in a box against the day someone asked." Accurate observation; outside the chapter's forward energy; interrupts the corridor walk.

**Keep:** "You will eat breakfast. There's a tray service for third class at seventh bell." Perfect. Reveals Theron's character, lands the cozy register, and earns its laugh without reaching for it.
