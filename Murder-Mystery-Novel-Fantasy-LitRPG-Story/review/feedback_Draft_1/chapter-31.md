# Chapter 31 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes, I would keep reading. The opening line — "Wednesday morning started in a thin grey light from the harbour side of the dining room and the kind of pot the host put down at first bell when the second loaves were still ten minutes off" — is slightly long and internally clause-stacked, but it grounds the reader immediately in texture and routine without explaining anything. The single line "He had not yet written anything new" earns attention because the case book is already active and the withholding feels deliberate, not coy. The first stumble is the paragraph describing Sable's hair: "the way she pulled it back when she had slept enough to bother, which was a smaller margin than not pulling it back at all and slept differently for it." That sentence is syntactically slippery on a first read — "slept differently for it" doesn't quite resolve — and nearly stopped me. Nothing else on page one broke the flow.

### 2. Planted details

No detail stopped me as a reader. The two Pessel quotations ("she could do the work after about three years, but Joren kept her seven more" / "he sent her south alone for the work that needed her hand more than his") land as professional shorthand, not as planted moments; in context they read as the case moving forward, which is correct. The observation about Sable's struck-out pencil note — "The line was not from this morning. The pencil was paler" — does create a small frisson of *she already worked this out and didn't say*, which is properly textural. Nothing here reads as pointed.

### 3. POV character

Theron is working well. He is precise, dry, self-aware about the distinction between "proven" and "fit," and not triumphant — the refusal to strike out last night's exclusivity-caveat, "because striking it out would have been the cheap pleasure of yesterday's certainty over today's," is exactly the character. The graduation-table aside works as self-knowledge rather than lecture. He is the right kind of insufferable: competent about his discipline, modest about his conclusions. Nothing here distanced me. I would spend more chapters with him.

### 4. Tone calibration

The tone holds as cozy-professional throughout. No dark passages, no gratuitous gravity. The one moment that briefly loses the register is this sentence: "He felt the specific quiet that came after a piece of structural work had finished placing itself on a page." The phrase "finished placing itself" is slightly too arch and has a faintly mechanical quality — the feeling being described is intimate, and the syntax is slightly too clever for the warmth the moment requires. Elsewhere the chapter is calibrated correctly — deliberate, working, no triumph, but not flat.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "She did not put it as a conclusion. She put it as a return to the line he had been waiting for her to put it as." The second sentence is an awkward reflexive; "waiting for her to put it as" stumbles at the close. The idea is present and sharp in the line it precedes; this setup sentence can go.

**Keep:** "The shape on the page had a person in it. The person was not on the page." Two sentences, nine words combined. Does the work without explaining it.
