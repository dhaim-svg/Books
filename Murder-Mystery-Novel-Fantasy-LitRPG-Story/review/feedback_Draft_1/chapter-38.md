# Chapter 38 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading. The opening is unusual enough to hold attention: the prose has a deliberate, rhythmic quality that signals a distinctive voice rather than generic fantasy. The doubled relative clause ("the way an inn took its evening occupants in a working harbour, which was without ceremony") could be trimmed without loss, but the surrounding room-as-a-lived-thing description earns its patience. The bread-board arrival and the three-word exchange ("Pie." / "Two." / "Two.") land cleanly and establish the register fast. Nothing on page one stopped me.

---

### 2. Planted details

One detail stood out: Sable putting her right hand into her coat pocket and "rested two fingers against the inside lining of the inside pocket where the carved fish had been since the twenty-eighth of October." This is the most explicitly pointed moment in the chapter. The named date ("since the twenty-eighth") combined with the specific pocket and the specific action made it feel flagged rather than textural — it paused me briefly. Everything else (the notebook, the pencil through the spine, the bread-cutting courtesy, the half-inch nod) felt completely natural and passed without stopping me.

---

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic, not distancing. Theron reads as thoughtful, slightly subdued — post-case rather than mid-case, which is a tonal shift from what a first-time reader might expect from an investigator protagonist, but it works. The notebook-closing scene is quiet and earned: the gap between what he wrote (the procedure) and what he carries (the case itself) is convincing characterization. The "half-inch" nod that he begins doing without noticing is a nice piece of social learning paid off in a small gesture. He feels like someone I'd follow for 100,000 words — careful, a little melancholy, not self-pitying.

---

### 4. Tone calibration

Strongly cozy throughout. The evening-inn scene, the bread, the stew-and-pie detail ("fish, potato, a little of the green that grew at the back of the kitchen garden"), the morning-harbour light, the dog watching the barman — all land in register. The notebook-closing scene is the most serious beat in the chapter and holds the cozy-but-not-light balance well: the weight is in the white space below "J. Aldis. Coastal. The work was not for himself," not in any authorial underlining. One possible flat note: the paragraph about the pie sits slightly outside Theron's interiority ("The pie sat in him in the way a hot meal sat in a person...") — the generalizing move ("in a person") distances briefly. Not damaging, just a slight slip from close third.

---

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The pie sat in him in the way a hot meal sat in a person who had eaten earlier than he would have eaten on the gondola and was, by the inn's clock, eating his second meal of an afternoon that had run long." — Too much load-bearing for a meal detail; the clause trail loses the flavour it's meant to deliver.

**Keep:** "He looked at the line he had written and at the white space below it. He had written the procedure. He had not written the case." — The rhythm is exact, and the distinction earns itself without commentary.
