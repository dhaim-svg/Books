# Chapter 06 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading. The opening room description does real work: the desk moved a hand's breadth, the chair turned to face the door. It tells you everything about Esherel before she speaks. "She did not stand. She did not offer the chair. She gestured at it. / 'Sit.'" is a perfect cozy-mystery opening beat. The Calden door-close line ("had developed a preference for the inaudible ones") earns a genuine smile. The only slight hesitation: "which was a thing he registered" — the construction is slightly precious, but it doesn't stop you. Nothing stalls the read on page one.

### 2. Planted details

No single detail stopped me as obviously planted. The folio's unfinished fourth paragraph lands as characterization, not as a clue — it accumulates slowly and pays off in the "composing the not-replying" passage, which is the chapter's best beat. The M. Tannin amendment shows up as a procedural thread the investigation is already tracking, so its payoff (paid on the dock) feels like forward motion, not planting. Calden's "with the captain's leave" stands out in a satisfying way — not planted so much as deliberately withheld, which the reader clocks as a narrative door to open later.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic, with texture. Theron reads as a bright young man running a method he trusts slightly too much — the reader can see he's categorizing everyone correctly and drawing exactly the wrong conclusions. "He filed her honesty as further data" is the line that crystallizes him: it's technically correct and slightly obtuse at once. The cabin note-taking scene — "he could feel his page accumulating. It was a satisfying feeling, slightly unwelcome" — is the best interiority of the chapter. Yes, 100,000 words with him is earned here.

### 4. Tone calibration

Solidly cozy throughout. The Henner-child-and-lemon-biscuits moment in the lounge is exactly the right texture break between interrogation beats. The tone never goes dark or clinical; even Esherel's grievance stays in the register of "this is a real person with a real annoyance, not a wound." The one wobble: the Calden pantry scene runs a beat or two past its natural end — the final "He went" is the right stop, but the paragraph before it re-summarizes what the reader just watched. Minor drag, doesn't break the register.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "He could feel his page accumulating. It was a satisfying feeling, slightly unwelcome." The second sentence overwrites what the image already does — "slightly unwelcome" names the dramatic irony the prose should carry without annotation.

**Keep:** *"I'd been looking forward to him not replying to it. I'd composed the not-replying. The not-replying was the point of the letter."* Esherel at her most precise. The triple repetition earns itself entirely.
