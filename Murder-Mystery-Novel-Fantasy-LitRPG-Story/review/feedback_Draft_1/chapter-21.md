# Chapter 21 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes, keep reading. The opening beats — the ink-brush noise, Theron's displacement of the slate exactly one inch, the TL-9 notation already on the table — establish a precise, controlled intelligence that makes the reader trust the prose immediately. The hook is oblique but earned: the copy was Sable's suggestion, and Theron has already internalized her reasoning before Fell arrives. The only mild friction is on first encounter with "TL-9 notation" — a reader new to the book needs a beat more context before that notation carries weight. It doesn't stop you; it just makes the first page slightly opaque before Fell's arrival grounds it.

### 2. Planted details

No planted character details land in this chapter per spec, and none arrive. What does register as slightly pointed: the moment Fell's hands move from flat-on-table to stacked in her lap after naming Berric Toll. It felt natural — an involuntary tell read at pace, not underlined. The Roven stillness is more staged by design, and it works as an interview beat rather than a plant. Nothing here stopped the reader or felt pointed-at.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic and interesting without being warm. Theron's self-awareness — "he let it run," referencing the Conservatory technique and catching himself calling it artificial — prevents him from reading as cold. The small acknowledgment that "she'd gotten there with less friction" lands as genuine respect rather than false modesty, and it keeps him likeable. The 100,000-word commitment question: yes. He's precise without being smug in this chapter, which is the right register for a long sit. No distancing moments.

### 4. Tone calibration

Holds the cozy register well for an interview scene — which risks reading as procedural flat. The bakery smell arriving in the debrief scene does real tonal work: it keeps the world warm even while the conversation is cool. One moment that tilts slightly: "The way she went still was not the way innocent people went still." This reads like a hard-boiled register intruding. It's a single sentence, and the following elaboration softens it, but the blunt verdict-first construction breaks the chapter's more observational mode.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The way she went still was not the way innocent people went still." — The sentence tells when the next three paragraphs show. It pre-empts the reader's read rather than trusting it.

**Keep:** "She had gathered it around herself like the well-kept coat, and it fit." — Earns its metaphor without overworking it. The coat was already on the page; the callback feels inevitable, not decorated.
