## Systemic Pattern 3 — Plant Discipline Flags

**Scope:** 3 chapters have plant-handling issues. None are full fails, but all need a revision pass before Draft_2.

### Ch 04 — Calluses re-description (borderline fail)
Spec says "do not re-describe [the callus] during the examination." The draft re-names the ridge with the exact Ch 01 language ("calloused index finger") framed as a confirmation filing rather than a fresh plant. This is defensible in prose terms but technically violates the spec's explicit instruction.
- **Decision:** Either (a) change the language so it reads as Theron filing "the same ridge" without repeating the Ch 01 description verbatim, or (b) explicitly authorize this usage in the spec as a "confirmation" category.

### Ch 13 — Carved fish callback too foregrounded
The carved fish is recalled on the stairwell landing with its own subordinate clause: "not in the luggage, which had been on the body." The spec says the five plants are "textural, not investigative" in this chapter. This callback makes the fish investigative — it's reasoning from the plant rather than passing it.
- **Fix:** Remove the subordinate clause. Let the fish be present in Theron's mind without the location reasoning attached. One sentence.

### Ch 38 — Carved fish date specificity
The carry-through names a specific date: "since the twenty-eighth of October." A first-time reader will clock this as investigative-grade specificity rather than textural memory.
- **Fix:** Remove the date or make it vaguer ("since the body"). The fish's significance to the case is already established — the date adds nothing the reader needs and over-specifies a plant that should stay ambient.

## Continuity Issue — Ch 35 Knowledge Leak

**Scope:** Single chapter, hard fix required.

**Issue:** In Ch 35 (Saturday, gondola), Sable says: *"I have the address through a separate post, with the magistrate's permission."* Per the known-facts ledger, Sable does not obtain the Joren family's home address until Ch 39 (from Pessel, Sunday morning in Sannholt). If this line means she has the family's direct parish address, it conflicts with Ch 39's on-page discovery moment.

**Fix:** Change the line to: *"I have a contact in Sannholt, through a separate post, with the magistrate's permission."* This makes clear she has Pessel's address (the intermediate contact she legitimately has at this point), not the family's address.
