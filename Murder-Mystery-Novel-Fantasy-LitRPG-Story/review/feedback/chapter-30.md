# Chapter 30 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — kept reading past page one without hesitation. The opening earns its keep immediately: the detail of the church bell ringing fractionally early on Tuesdays is specific enough to feel real and characterising without being showy. The economy of "He had been sitting at the table since the soup. The soup had been four hours ago." does the job of a much longer setup in two sentences. The host's pre-emption of every room-and-food concern before Sable asks nails the cozy register. The arrival scene is warm and unhurried in exactly the right way — it delays the actual case work without feeling like stalling, because the physical business of a tired traveller arriving is interesting in itself.

### 2. Planted details

Nothing flagged as a planted detail in this chapter. The technical information about the glamour exploit (seating phase, ambient field, trigger distance) is laid out at length, but it reads as investigative exposition rather than a planted clue — the reader is clearly meant to follow it, not file it away. The corridor measurement ("thirty feet of corridor") is the closest thing to a planted beat, but it arrives in context and gets used immediately; it passes as working data, not as a future hook. Nothing stopped the read.

### 3. POV character

Theron lands well here. The detail of "watching her eat without watching her eat, which was a small recent skill he had been developing without comment" is exactly the right register — affectionate, slightly self-aware, and funny in a quiet way that didn't demand acknowledgement. He reads as someone who has been waiting patiently for something to happen and is now doing real work at last. The restraint at the end — "He liked that about it. He did not say so to anyone." — is nicely observed. Happy to spend 100,000 words with him. The slight insufferability of a prodigy being right is present but controlled.

### 4. Tone calibration

Solidly cozy beach-read throughout. The arrival scene is warm and grounded. The working-table sequence picks up the right register: two people doing careful intellectual work late at night in a pub that is closing around them. The chapter's emotional weight comes from the scale of what the theory explains — a man died, this is why — and the draft carries that without tipping into grimness. The host putting up chairs in the background is doing real atmospheric work. No moment felt wrong. The System screen at the close is brief, laconic, and tonally matched to the surrounding prose. The closest risk point is the extended technical exposition (seating phase, trigger mechanics), which could have gone flat — it doesn't, mainly because Sable's questioning keeps it from becoming a lecture.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "She had been the one who had logged the corridor measurement on the original Wyrm interview sheet, three weeks ago, in her own hand, in the column where the steward's pass-by notes lived." The attributive chain does unnecessary work — the reader doesn't need the provenance footnote to feel the recognition land.

**Keep exactly:** "He had been sitting at the table since the soup. The soup had been four hours ago."
