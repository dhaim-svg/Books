# Chapter 01 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes, keep reading. The opening line earns immediate goodwill — the precise observation that eleven people all arrived at first bell and are now "looking at different things to avoid acknowledging this" is sharp, funny, and economical. It establishes the POV voice in one beat. The drake introduction lands well and the world feels lived-in rather than explained. The only hesitation comes just before Sable's appearance: the paragraph listing the boarding crowd (family, scholars, courier, Madam Esherel) stacks names and impressions at a pace that slightly slows momentum. It does not stop reading — but it is the one moment where the chapter feels like it is settling in rather than moving.

### 2. Planted details

Two details registered:

**The calluses** — felt natural. The handshake observation slips by cleanly; the "noble who dabbles / practices more seriously than that implies" filing reads like a character quirk, not a pointing arrow. Passed without stopping.

**Vannic thanking Calden** — also natural. The woman in the brown coat going still in the doorway drew momentary attention (it is clearly significant), but the prose does not underline it — it holds back and moves on. A first-time reader will notice the stillness has a quality and file it. It does not feel planted; it feels like a human moment the narrator happened to catch.

No other detail registered as suspicious.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic and interesting. Theron is a specific kind of pleasant insufferable — the tugging at the pin, the memorized gondola specifications, the cabin-checking ritual — and the details feel earned rather than stated. The paragraph about his parents (mother who cried, father who said *good* in the voice that means more than good) cracks the armour briefly in exactly the right way. He lands as someone to spend time with: precise, a bit pleased with himself, but not cruel, and with enough self-awareness to know about the six-months-of-checking-the-pin problem. 100,000 words — yes.

### 4. Tone calibration

Cozy, and correctly so. The tone is stable throughout: light, image-led, unhurried in the best sense. The only moment that tested the register was the porthole lift-off passage, where the lamp oil triggering a memory of the apothecary cabinet risks tipping from nostalgic warmth into something heavier. It does not cross the line — "noted it and moved on" holds the register — but it is the closest the chapter comes to loading the ending with more weight than the opening chapters need. The closing image (pin set down, Petal banking through the air) is pitch-perfect cozy.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The layout settled into him the way layouts did — stairways, outer deck hatches, window line of the first-class corridor above — filed and available, taking up no more space than it needed." The em-dash list slows a transitional beat that doesn't need it; the insight is already carried by his other behavior.

**Keep:** "She banked through the air with the ease of something that had never had reason to question whether it belonged there." Clean, earns its place, resonates beyond Petal without forcing the parallel.
