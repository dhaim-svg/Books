# Chapter 07 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading past page one. The opening line earns its weight immediately: "Theron came down to the steward's station to ask the captain's leave and found the leave already in progress without him." That's a setup, a complication, and a small social humiliation compressed into one sentence. The eavesdropping beat that follows is clean and specific; Calden's register-shift ("a man closing a drawer that had been open") is earned and image-led rather than explained. Nothing stopped forward momentum on page one. The one hesitation: the phrase "category problem of his own" in the dining-room section reads slightly academic for in-the-moment interiority, but it doesn't halt the reader — it's just the most clinical phrase in an otherwise observational chapter.

### 2. Planted details

One detail stood out: the accent slip. "He reached for a word at dinner the first night — a perfectly ordinary word, *harbor* — and the vowel came out flat. Coastal flat." This landed as **natural**. Sable frames it as something a well-bred person would notice and not say, which is the exact explanation a non-suspicious observer would offer themselves. The beat passes without feeling pointed. The steward's "half-second slower" observation also stood out — but this read as **characterization of Sable and Calden**, not as planted evidence about the victim. No detail in this chapter stopped me or made me think "that detail will matter."

### 3. POV character

Theron lands as sympathetic and slightly insufferable in exactly the right proportion. The sequence where he reaches for a question about Sable's observation and hears "in his own head, the sound of the question landing wrong" before he asks it is his best moment — self-correction without self-flagellation. The small mortified heat at the back of his neck when he realizes he'd filed her wrong is pitch-perfect. He earns spending 100,000 words with him here. One minor note: his decision to stop performing for Sable during the meal is told via "He stopped performing" — a filtering statement. It would be stronger as a behavior: a moment he reaches for bread without thinking, or lets a silence go where he'd normally fill it.

### 4. Tone calibration

This reads as cozy beach-read throughout. The chapter has the right texture: leftover soup that "had decided it was now soup," migrating lemon biscuits, coffee that "had stood." The pacing is unhurried without being slow — three scenes, each with a distinct spatial and conversational gear. No moment drifts too dark or too jokey. The closest thing to a tone wobble is the corridor scene's final image: "looking at the line her body had made across the boards." Slightly more elegiac than the rest of the chapter's register, but it doesn't break cozy; it deepens it appropriately at the close.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "He had not heard a person say a steward's *body* in a sentence that did not mean *corpse*, and he realized in the same beat that this was a category problem of his own, not of hers." The word "category problem" over-explains what the rest of the sentence already shows. The thought would be stronger as two shorter beats ending at "not of hers."

**Keep exactly as written:** "Calden thanks people who thank him by name. Not formally — internally. You can see it because he goes a half-second slower the next time he comes back."
