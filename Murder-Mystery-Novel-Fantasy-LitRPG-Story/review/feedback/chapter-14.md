## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading past page one. The opening is precise and immediately character-defining: a man composing his opening sentence before he knocks, having discarded three alternatives on the stairs. That is efficient characterisation through action rather than description. The payoff of Theron actually *using* the rehearsed line — and Esherel's compressed, unwilling laugh at hearing it — lands the dynamic between them in two paragraphs. Nothing stopped the read. The one moment that slightly slows is "She was in her day dress but her hair was down, which he registered and immediately set aside as not his business" — the parenthetical self-correction is charming but it does pause forward momentum for a beat of domestic observation that isn't quite earned yet.

The exchange "You spent three days watching me" / "Yes" is exactly the right register for him: honest, unapologetic, not performatively cold. The admission that he should have come to Esherel earlier ("You were rank one" / "I know I was rank one") acknowledges failure without melodrama. The 100,000-word question: yes, this character is sustainable. He has a recognisable cognitive style and enough self-awareness to be correctable. Esherel's "You're better at this than I thought you were going to be" lands as genuine calibration, not flattery, which means the reader trusts it.

### 4. Tone calibration

Beach-read throughout. The cold coffee served as either a courtesy or an armistice is exactly the right register — light, specific, a little wry. Esherel's pivot from grievance to useful source is handled without grief or melodrama; the tone stays brisk. The Sable-and-manifest section shifts gear into procedural but remains readable because the stakes are shaped (fourteen names, four blanks, one shared port). The one potential flat moment is the oilcloth-packet section, which risks feeling like an information delivery. It earns its place through Theron's noted deliberateness about not opening the packet until he had the right questions — that internal beat keeps it from feeling mechanical. The closing image (the gondola shifting on a thermal, the Halveth coastline appearing) is well-placed. No tone violations.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "She settled into the chair across from him, not the armchair, the straight-backed one she'd been using for correspondence, and set her coffee cup on the arm of it." — The double chair specification (not the armchair / the straight-backed one) does too much work flagging that Esherel is not relaxing. The "set her coffee cup on the arm of it" adds nothing after the chair detail has already done its job.

**Keep exactly as written:** "She was quiet for a moment, in the way of someone weighing something they hadn't meant to say."
