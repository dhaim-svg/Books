# Chapter 34 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading. The opening paragraph's inventory of the cabin (porthole, desk, lamp bracket angled for reading, honest wardrobe) works because it is not decoration; it is Theron reading a room the way Theron reads rooms. The sentence "The lamp bracket had been angled by someone who had thought about reading in bed" does the job quietly. The first real pause is the line "He thought briefly about the page that was not in the book" — at that point it becomes clear the narrator is working around something, and that mystery is enough to hold a reader through to the knock on cabin six.

### 2. Planted details

Nothing stopped me. One detail registered as slightly unusual: Halsa keeps her left hand in her coat pocket the entire meeting and only removes it when the emotional peak arrives (p. 13). As a first-time reader I noticed this — a contained physicality that felt like either a character habit or something specific to the scene — but it did not feel pointed. It passed as nervous composure. The sealed note from the magistrate that she has not opened reads as natural; it carries weight without underlining it. No detail in this chapter felt planted in the spoiler-foreshadowing sense.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic, with restraint. Theron does not gloat, does not press, does not perform competence. The chapter shows him making quiet decisions — tying the strap not because it needs tying but as a ritual for *now* — and those small acts of self-discipline land as character, not annotation. The Sable dynamic (she is there, she speaks four words, she stays at the wall) works because his awareness of her is incidental rather than worshipful. Spendable for 100,000 words: yes, without reservation.

### 4. Tone calibration

Cozy, but with earned gravity. This is the confrontation chapter, and it does not flinch into sentimentality or darken into gloom. The System line — *No contestation logged. Statement under writ: pending* — is laconic and right. One moment where tone wobbled slightly: the sentence "He had said in Davren's office that the case book would carry..." opens the second paragraph with a long, clause-heavy structure that accumulates in a way the rest of the chapter does not. It is not wrong, but it is the chapter's slowest sentence.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "He had said in Davren's office that the case book would carry the abstract, the scry log, the transcripts, the manifest. He had laid it out that way at first bell on Friday and again at sixth bell yesterday in his own room at the boarding-house, when he had set the leather strap on his bed and gone through it page by page in the way a man went through a bag for a journey he meant to take cleanly." The recapping of the case-book contents is established again moments later; these two sentences front-load rather than open.

**Keep:** "The voice did not waver and did not perform not-wavering, which were different things."
