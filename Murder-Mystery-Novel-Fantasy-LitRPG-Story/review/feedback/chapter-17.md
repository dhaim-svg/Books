# Chapter 17 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes — keep reading. The opening hook is quiet but confident: a knock, two coat pockets checked, the door answered. That small ritual communicates a man who prepares before he acts, and it earns the read. The magistrate's entrance ("compact, somewhere in her fifties") is specific enough to trust. The first real speed bump is the dense expository block beginning "The deceased is Joren Aldis" — Theron reciting the case facts reads as a report rather than scene, which slows the rhythm. A new reader, not yet anchored to the plot, may skim it. It is not a put-down-the-book problem, but the momentum noticeably dips before recovering with "Halved it."

### 2. Planted details

No planted details were noticeable as planted in this chapter. One beat that caught attention: the italicized System block near the end — *Investigator — Status: Fell thread active. One address in Sannholt, one board list to check. The case is not tidier than it was. It is more accurate.* This reads as character-voiced rather than mechanical, and it passed naturally. It did not stop the read; it felt like an earned internal marker. No other detail registered as pointed.

### 3. POV character

Theron lands well. His stance throughout — precise in the magistrate scene, silently present in the debt scene, methodical at the quayside — is consistent and readable. The line "He looked back at him in a manner that communicated clearly that he had been introduced as a witness, not a resource" is the chapter's best character-beat: dry, self-aware, shows restraint rather than stating it. Not immediately loveable, but you would spend a book with him. He is interesting rather than endearing, which is the right register for this genre.

### 4. Tone calibration

Overwhelmingly well-calibrated. The magistrate scene is brisk without being cold; the debt scene lands exactly in the cozy-uncomfortable register — Vannic holding the pen while Esherel waits with the patience of "a woman who had been waiting eighteen months for a specific page to be opened" is a small perfect beat. The harbor scene has the right amount of procedural texture without tipping into plodding. One possible miscalibration: the closing image ("Then it didn't") after the dock call across the water is good atmospherically but slightly melancholic — it may land darker than intended for a beach-read close. Not a serious problem, but worth noting.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The magistrate herself came in behind the aide." The word *herself* reads as emphasis without payoff — she's the subject of the paragraph regardless, and the qualifier slows entry.

**Keep exactly as written:** "He looked at it for a moment. Theron had the sense that this was the first time, in the entire run of things, that Vannic had a clear lane ahead of him — one action, specific, no betrothal, no dead illusionist, no identity documents to maintain. Just a pen and a number he knew was correct."
