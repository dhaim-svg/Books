The briefing scene moves with a quiet procedural energy that suits the cozy-investigative register. ### 2. Planted details

Two details stood out as slightly unusual in a way that reads as textural rather than pointed:

- Halsa Renn answering in the doorway without stepping back. Noticed as a small character habit, passed without underlining. Natural.
- Renn's observation that Calden "had a particular walk" and that it changed on the third morning. Slightly unusual — this is a precise behavioral read from a woman Theron is treating as unremarkable. It landed as character flavor (she's observant) rather than as a planted clue. Natural, but the quietest edge of pointed — one careful reader in ten might pause here.

Neither stopped me. The textile buyer's "she looks like someone doing a job" about Fell also registered as a useful character note rather than a flag.

### 3. POV character

Theron works well here. He is precise without being cold — the small detail of circling Fell's name "not because it needed circling, but because the motion helped him think" does a lot to humanize him cheaply. The chapter's version of him is problem-solving rather than showing off, which is more likeable than a prodigy in full performance mode. The filing of Renn as fourth-tier, done without drama and without lingering, is convincing as his way of thinking. For a chapter this procedural, his voice keeps it from reading as a mere checklist. Would spend 100,000 words with him.

### 4. Tone calibration

Mostly correct: cozy, close, specific. The corridor-light description ("warm but not bright, full of small sounds") is the right register. The grieving man in cabin five-T is handled precisely — the detail of the empty sleeping-draught vial, the small wanting-to-do-the-right-thing — lands without tipping into darkness. The only soft flat spot is the Fell interview: it is technically clean but runs slightly long for a scene whose conclusion is "no line drawn." The repeat beat of "her answers were clean and specific and added up to a picture of someone with nothing to hide" doesn't need the follow-up sentence about "the profile of someone with the right background and the patience to know that the cleanest accounting tends to pass earliest" — that's Theron making the same observation twice. A small tone dip into over-explanation.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The harbor connection to Toll was real enough to hold. The professional description fit. Nothing she had given him was inconsistent with a woman who worked near the same port and had no involvement in whatever had been arranged on the west side of it." — This is Theron re-summarizing what the reader just witnessed. The scene has already shown it; the recap undercuts the interview's texture.

**Keep exactly as written:** "I'm sorry about your brother," Sable said, from the doorway. / The man looked at her. His expression was the kind that doesn't have a quick answer and doesn't try to find one. "Thank you," he said." — Two lines, no excess. The grief lands without commentary.
