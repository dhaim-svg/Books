# Chapter 12 — Review

## Blind Review

### 1. First-page test

Yes, keep reading. The opening line earns its length — "the page in his pocket had decided overnight that it wanted finishing, and he had decided over the same hours not to argue with it" establishes character voice immediately and creates forward momentum without backstory dump. The bow-window coffee scene on the second paragraph is a brief stumble: "the way a person stood at a kitchen window in a house he had lived in for a year" stacks simile on simile (long flight + kitchen window) and momentarily floats free of the gondola setting. It costs one breath of attention but does not stop the read. By the third paragraph — the listed page items, the absent underline — the chapter has the reader's trust. No stopping point on page one.

### 2. Planted details

Nothing in this chapter stopped me and felt pointed. The cup-handle quarter-turn from Sable (twice noted) is the most prominent recurring gesture — I noticed it was being tracked by the narrator and wondered whether it carried significance, but the prose does not underline it, so it passed as character texture. The secondary harness note, mentioned twice, reads as atmospheric world-building rather than a planted clue. The mother's uneven shirt stitches feel purely characterful. Nothing in the chapter reads as a planted detail being aimed at the reader.

### 3. POV character

Sympathetic and distinctive. The prose habit of completing a thought and then auditing it — "he was aware of which of those was the careful answer, and he was also aware, in the small honest part of his attention" — quickly establishes a character who is self-correcting rather than self-congratulatory. He is precise without being cold. The moment he puts on the good shirt and runs his thumb along his mother's stitching is the warmest beat in the chapter and lands without sentimentality. He is someone worth 100,000 words: methodical, quietly funny, and in the process of becoming something slightly less armored than he started.

### 4. Tone calibration

Cozy at consistent peak — this reads exactly like a beach-read that has earned the reader's full comfort. No moment tips dark. The Vellaine exchange is the chapter's tonal highlight: dry, warm, mutual, brief. The supper scene with Sable is the right length and does not overstay; the cottage-inheritance digression earns its place as intimacy-by-indirection. One small flatness: the corridor sightline check at the open of Scene 2 is technical and functional — it does exactly what it needs to do, but it sits slightly cooler than everything surrounding it. That is probably deliberate pacing and not a flaw.

### 5. Line-level

**Cut:** "The bow window was already at the brass urn. Two of the second-watch passengers were standing at it the way people stood at a long forward window on the tenth morning of a flight — comfortably, without commenting on the view, the way a person stood at a kitchen window in a house he had lived in for a year."
The doubled simile (flight/kitchen) does unnecessary work and the whole sentence can reduce to "the bow window had its usual knot of second-watch passengers at the brass urn" without loss.

**Keep:** "The underline took the slate seriously. The slate took the underline seriously back."
