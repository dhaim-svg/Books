# Internal Audit — Chapter 3

## Wisp Pronouns

All pronoun references to Wisp in Ch. 3.

1. "Wisp was inside, sitting on an upended crate with a coil of harness chain in her lap, working a wax-impregnated cloth along the links one by one."
   [Ch. 3, para ~7 (rigging house scene), pronoun = her]

2. "Wisp doesn't look up."
   — No pronoun in this sentence.

3. "She rotated the chain a half-link and went on cleaning."
   [Ch. 3, para ~9, pronoun = she]

4. "She held a link up to the light, turned it, set it back in the lap."
   [Ch. 3, para ~16, pronoun = she]

5. "She pulled the door wider with her shoulder."
   [Ch. 3, para ~18, pronoun = she / her]

6. "She doesn't need a theory."
   [Ch. 3, para ~13, pronoun = she — referring to Petal, not Wisp; excluded from Wisp count]

7. "She always does." (Calden quoting Wisp's habit about fourth watch)
   [Ch. 3, para ~30, pronoun = she — attributed to Wisp by implication]

**Summary:** Ch. 3 uses female pronouns (she/her) for Wisp throughout — reverting to Ch. 1's usage and contradicting Ch. 2's male pronouns. Confirmed instances: "her lap" (para ~7), "She rotated" (para ~9), "She held" (para ~16), "She pulled...her shoulder" (para ~18), and "She always does" in Calden's remark (para ~30). The pronoun is inconsistent across all three chapters: Ch. 1 = she/her, Ch. 2 = he/him/his, Ch. 3 = she/her.
