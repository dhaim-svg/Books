## Load-Bearing Chapters (cannot cut or merge)

- **Ch. 23:** Contains the frame-shift formalisation (wrong question acknowledged in writing) AND the Norra Esherel corridor scene — both are structurally required. The fish's pre-death moment establishes its emotional weight before it is handed off. Cannot be cut.
- **Ch. 25:** The handoff of the carved fish to Sable is the central symbolic action of the entire investigation arc; the Marbeth/Kolach thread advances materially. Cannot be cut, though the opening (fourth clerk scene in three chapters) is compressible.
- **Ch. 26:** Renn's name appearing in the Kolach placement log is the case's first hard turn toward the correct suspect. The Sable-Pessel apprentice preview (landlady detail) is the other shoe. Indispensable.
- **Ch. 29:** The thesis retrieval is the method breakthrough — structurally the most original scene in the block, and it earns the working theory that the next two chapters depend on. Cannot be cut.
- **Ch. 30:** The cabin-geometry test and scry-log reinterpretation convert the working theory from speculative to structurally consistent. Sable's sentence (invisible-method insight) is load-bearing for the case's logic. Cannot be cut.
- **Ch. 31:** The six-requirements structural list completes the "who fits the method" argument, and the Pessel detail about the seven-year apprenticeship (trigger architecture specifically taught) is essential. Cannot be cut.

## Compressible / Mergeable Chapters

- **Ch. 24:** Could compress to ~65% of current length. The wrong-frame reflection on Renn repeats Ch. 23's frame-shift beat without adding new emotional register. The Calden notebook handoff is load-bearing (lamp observation, Renn flag) but the breakfast routine and Sable-departure material is partly repeated in Ch. 25. Could shed the second wrong-frame meditation and one harbour-through-window passage.
- **Ch. 27:** The most compressible chapter in the block. Pessel's testimony is essential, but this chapter reads as: (a) Theron drafts profile (two pages), (b) Sable's letter arrives (three pages), (c) Theron writes reply (one page), (d) Sunday harbour. The profile draft has no new content not implied by Ch. 26. Could compress to ~60% by cutting the profile-drafting sequence and reducing the reply to its essential lines, delivering the content in one movement: letter arrives, key facts extracted, reply sent.
- **Ch. 28:** The Davren scene is load-bearing, but the chapter has two flanking sequences (post-Davren case-book session, post-transcript case-book session) that are near-identical in structure. Could merge the two flanking sequences into one, cutting to ~75% of current length.
- **Chs. 24 and 27 are the strongest merge candidates.** Ch. 24 closes with Sable departing and two open threads (warrant, re-exam). Ch. 27 could absorb its own essential content — Pessel's letter and the Q4 question — into the space vacated by cutting Ch. 24's repeated frame-shift reflection. A merged chapter would run the re-exam correction, the Calden notes, and Sable's departure into one first half, then time-jump to the Pessel letter arriving, cutting the Sunday-morning profile draft entirely.

## Repeating Scene Types

1. **Investigator at Arms corner table, case book open, coffee present** — appears in every chapter of the block (9 of 9 chapters). The table is the default setting for all thinking, writing, and receiving of information.
2. **Clerk or courier arrives at the Arms table with new information** — Ch. 23 (magistrate's clerk, registry notation), Ch. 25 (magistrate's clerk, warrant), Ch. 26 (clerk, wire summary; then post-boy, Sable's letter), Ch. 28 (post-boy, partial transcript; then courier note), Ch. 29 (post-boy, Sable's road letter). Five distinct arrivals, four of which involve the same physical beat: Theron at table, boy or clerk enters, document handed over, document read twice.
3. **Theron writes in the case book / reads the page back / closes the case book** — explicit at the close of Chs. 23, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30, and 31. In seven of nine chapters this is the final action before the chapter ends.
4. **Letter-arrives, read-twice, write-reply, seal-for-coach** — Chs. 26, 27 (full Pessel letter), 28 (courier note), 29 (road letter). Four consecutive chapters with this rhythm.
5. **Harbour-through-window description as chapter punctuation** — Chs. 23, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30, 31. Present in every chapter; varies slightly (morning light, Saturday business, rope-master, Monday speed) but the beat is structurally identical each time.
6. **Wrong-frame self-critique reflection** — Chs. 23 and 24 both open with extended passages in which Theron examines how his training filtered out the correct suspect. Functionally the same content in two consecutive chapters.
7. **System annotation at chapter close** — Chs. 23, 24, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30, 31. Eight of nine chapters end with a bracketed System line that recaps what the chapter has accomplished. These are individually appropriate but, at this density, collectively flatten the variation between chapters' emotional registers.

## Emotional High Point

**Ch. 23** — Norra Esherel's report of the corridor scene: Joren in the first-class passage at second watch, taking the carved fish from his outer pocket, looking at it in private, putting it away, walking on to dinner. The delivery is controlled (Norra speaking for Fellin, who sent a messenger because she "didn't want to make it about her feelings"), and Theron's response (closing the case book, writing "Not afraid. Himself.") is the most human moment in the block. The scene does not advance the plot and it does not need to.

The handoff of the fish to Sable at the coach yard (Ch. 25) is a close second.

## Lowest-Energy Stretch

**Ch. 27 (in full) through the first half of Ch. 28.** This is where the "table/coffee/list/clerk/coach" complaint is most accurate.

Ch. 27 runs: profile draft (Theron writing a column we already have in outline from Ch. 26) → Sable's letter arrives (essential content: Pessel's testimony) → Theron writes reply → Sunday harbour → Theron re-reads profile → close. The Pessel testimony is necessary and well-written, but it is surrounded by a chapter's worth of apparatus — the profile draft that pre-empts it, the leisurely reply, the Sunday harbour repeat — that produces no forward movement. A reader who has absorbed Ch. 26 is already ahead of the profile. The chapter asks them to wait.

Ch. 28's first half (pre-Davren) and the extended post-transcript sitting that closes it repeat this beat: case book open, case book considered, nothing written. The Davren scene itself is brisk and productive; it is the surrounding padding that sags.

---

## Recommended Compression Approach

Cut Ch. 27's profile-drafting sequence entirely (its content is redundant with the Ch. 26 column) and open the chapter on the Pessel letter arriving, so the reader reaches the testimony without delay. Compress Ch. 24 by removing the second wrong-frame reflection, since Ch. 23 has already performed that beat; keep the Calden-notebook handoff and Sable's departure, which are load-bearing. Trim Ch. 28 to a single flanking sequence around the Davren meeting rather than two: one scene before, one scene after, not three case-book-open moments. The block's harbour-through-window punctuation should appear no more than three or four times across nine chapters — rotate in one or two different atmospheric registers (the coach yard, the stair, the corridor outside Theron's room) so the window does not become a structural tic. These four adjustments would reduce total block length by roughly 15-20% and, more importantly, would break the chapter-shaped sameness that makes each installment feel interchangeable — restoring the sense that arriving at a new chapter means arriving somewhere new.
