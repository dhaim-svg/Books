## Opening Pacing (Ch. 1 ONLY)

Ch. 1 is front-weighted with cozy texture — the drake pre-boarding exchange with Wisp, the queue filling in increments, the cabin inspection, and the liftoff porthole scene occupy the full chapter before any mystery premise surfaces. The first beat that moves the story toward its mystery premise is Vannic's introduction (the crowd-hush, the subtle Esherel reaction, the handshake callus that Theron files as more than a hobby), which appears roughly in the chapter's second third. The slice-of-life texture earns most of its runtime: the Wisp scene does double duty as world-building and character voice, Theron's methodical cabin-settling establishes his classifying mind, and the boarding-queue social dynamics introduce the supporting cast efficiently. The delay is not materially harmful for a cozy LitRPG — the tone contract with the reader is set before the hook is needed — though the chapter does not land a single forward-pulling question by its close; the sole dangling note (the woman in the brown coat going still at Vannic's thank-you) is suggestive but very quiet.
