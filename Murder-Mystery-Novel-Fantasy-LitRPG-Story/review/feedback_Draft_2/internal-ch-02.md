# Internal Audit — Chapter 2

## Wisp Pronouns

All pronoun references to Wisp in Ch. 2.

1. "Wisp appeared with a tension gauge and looked at the formation, then at Theron."
   — No pronoun in this sentence; introduction only.

2. "He made a second small notation."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9 (promenade scene), pronoun = he]

3. "He said it with complete seriousness."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9, pronoun = he]

4. "He capped the gauge, and for a moment they both watched Petal hold her line..."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9, pronoun = he]

5. "Wisp tucked the gauge under his arm."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9, pronoun = his]

6. "He said it without affection, or with the kind of affection that had been so thoroughly absorbed into observation it no longer showed as a separate thing."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9, pronoun = he]

7. "He keeps it at the dining-room end in the mornings."
   [Ch. 2, para ~9 — Wisp's closing line directing Theron to coffee, pronoun = he]

**Summary:** Ch. 2 uses male pronouns (he/his/him) for Wisp throughout — six pronoun instances, all masculine. This is in direct conflict with Ch. 1's female pronouns (she/her).

## Flight Duration

1. "*[No active queries. 11 hours to Halverstow Port.]*"
   [Ch. 2, para ~22 (reading-alcove System message), says: "11 hours to Halverstow Port"]

**Summary:** The System message in Ch. 2 gives a remaining flight time of 11 hours. This contradicts Ch. 1's established voyage length of "three days." Even accounting for one night of travel between Ch. 1 and Ch. 2, a three-day trip would have roughly 48+ hours remaining at this point in the narrative, not 11. The figures are irreconcilable without revision to one or both passages.
