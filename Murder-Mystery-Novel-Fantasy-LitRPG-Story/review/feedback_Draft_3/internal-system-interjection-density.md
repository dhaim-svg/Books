# Internal Audit — System Interjection Density (Ch.2, 3, 10, 12, 17, 28, 40)

## Convention reference

Per `bible/standing-style.md`: System screens should be **sparse** — used only when they earn the interruption. They are formatted as indented italic blocks; Theron's relationship to them is the mild satisfaction of a graduate whose training is paying off, not wonder or boredom. The System is not a narrator. It does not editorialize on behalf of the reader. It speaks in the idiom of a professional tool: concise, probabilistic, confirmatory. Anything the prose can carry through Theron's own observation, the System should not carry.

## Cross-chapter pattern

Three patterns have drifted from the original convention:

**1. The closing aphorism (Ch.10, Ch.12, Ch.17)**
The System has developed a habit of closing chapter beats with a philosophical summary of what the chapter has shown. "The cleanest page is the page you didn't write yet." "A column closes when the careful hand stops adding to it." "The case is not tidier than it was. It is more accurate." These are well-written sentences, but they perform a captioning function — the System arriving after a scene beat to tell the reader what they've just witnessed. This is the opposite of the device's intent. The System should confirm or record; it should not interpret. In all three cases, the prose has already delivered the insight more powerfully through action or image. The aphorism deadens rather than amplifies.

**2. The countdown pair (Ch.10, Ch.12)**
*Quest active. [N] hours, [M] minutes to Halverstow Port* appears in consecutive chapters at the same structural position (cabin → pin gesture → exit to dinner). The repetition creates a formulaic rhythm — the System as chapter clock. The minute-precision of the countdown tips both toward HUD-style delivery (the bible explicitly warns against HUD-style System boxes). One instance might be earned; two instances back-to-back read as reflex rather than intent.

**3. The confidence-boost pattern**
Several messages arrive when Theron has reached an investigative conclusion and appears to need validation: the Insight +1 in Ch.2 (Vellaine recognized him), the Insight +1 in Ch.10 (don't write the conclusion), the Insight +1 in Ch.12 (the column is closed). The Insight +1 framing specifically reads as gamified reward for correct play — the System praising Theron for good discipline. This is close to the "wonder" register the bible prohibits. Theron is supposed to have the mild satisfaction of someone whose training is paying off, not the satisfaction of being awarded points. The Insight +1 tag in Ch.2 is least problematic because the content is informational (Vellaine has read the paper). The tags in Ch.10 and Ch.12 are purely evaluative.

## Climax payoff (Ch.35)

The Reader-praised System message is in Ch.35:

> *[Investigator — The question has been asked. The question has been answered. The room has set down what it brought.]*

This earns its place for several reasons that the mid-book messages do not:

- It arrives after the most emotionally demanding scene in the novel, when Halsa has asked whether Joren's real name was used, and Sable has answered. The System does not summarize the scene. It does not aphorize it. It describes the shape of what has happened in the simplest possible terms — *question asked, question answered, room has set down what it brought* — which is a different register from captioning. It is witnessing.
- It names nothing that the reader doesn't already know. It adds nothing interpretive. It is the System's version of the "small dry nod" Theron gives it — professional acknowledgement of a thing concluded.
- Theron's response is perfect: "He read the line through once. He gave it the small dry nod he had given the System every morning of the week." This is the graduation. Not wonder. Not boredom. The dry nod of someone whose training has been paying off for the length of the novel.

The payoff reads as earned **precisely because** the System has been sparse and dry elsewhere. The issue is that the mid-book aphorism cluster (Ch.10, Ch.12, Ch.17) risks making this closing message feel like one more in a series of philosophical summaries rather than the earned single moment it actually is.

**Thinning the mid-book messages strengthens the climax payoff.** If Ch.10's "The cleanest page is the page you didn't write yet" and Ch.12's "A column closes when the careful hand stops adding to it" are cut, the Ch.35 message stands as the first time the System speaks in that reflective register. The payoff's quality does not come from its phrasing alone — it comes from the contrast between its warmth and the cool factual register everything before it has been in. Saturating the mid-book chapters with aphorisms corrodes that contrast.

The Ch.40 message (*note: the corner of the slip*) is the post-climax use and is correctly calibrated: five words, no interpretation, pure confirmation. It does not compete with Ch.35 because it operates in a completely different register.

## Preserve list

The amendment is the seed of the mystery. The System is the correct vehicle for it — an administrative fact surfaced by a professional tool, not a deduction Theron makes. Must stay.

The single System message in Ch.3 does exactly what the device is for: it confirms a decision Theron has already made, in the System's dry register, and makes him smile. There is no earlier prose beat it doubles. Keep.

The column-update function is legitimate here — it acts as case-record acknowledgement at the structural hinge when Theron has the *who* and has named the *method* problem. After trimming the editorial note, this is a lean and correct use of the device.

**Ch.35, single message** (*The question has been asked. The question has been answered. The room has set down what it brought.*)
The Reader-praised climax payoff. Untouchable. This is the message the rest of the device's usage in the book is building toward, and it earns its place by witnessing rather than interpreting. Do not alter.

**Ch.40, single message** (*note: the corner of the slip.*)
Five words. No aphorism. Pure case-record confirmation at the exact moment the device should confirm and not explain. A model use. Keep.
