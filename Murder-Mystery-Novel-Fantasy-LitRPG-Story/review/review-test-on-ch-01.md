## Test `review-brief-blind.md`
The chapter content is already in context. Here's the developmental edit.

1. First-page test
Yes, I'd keep reading. The woman in the brown coat, motionless at the door gap — natural enough on first pass, but the paragraph spends four sentences on her stillness, which is a lot of attention for a character who hasn't been named. Registered as significant, not jarring.

The graduation pin (repeated touching) — entirely natural. New-thing nervous habit, not foregrounded.

3. POV character
Theron works. The dry internal voice, the filing-and-moving-on cognitive style, the self-awareness about the pin ("he was also aware that it would take roughly six months to stop checking") are all likeable without being cute. The father's "good" landing — "meant something longer than good" — is the emotional anchor the chapter needed and earns genuine warmth.

One flag: he's almost entirely an observing instrument in this chapter, with no present want beyond "rest." That's fine for an opening, but if his only interiority is noticing and filing, he risks feeling like a narrative camera rather than a person by chapter three.

4. Tone calibration

The lamp oil / dispensary memory: elegiac rather than cozy. Not wrong, but heavier than the chapter's established key.
"She banked through the air with the ease of something that had never had reason to question whether it belonged there." — too wistful for a beach-read landing. Reads like it's reaching for thematic resonance the chapter hasn't yet set up.
The System notification ([Investigator — Field status: Unscheduled interval.]) is perfectly calibrated: brief, dry, doesn't interrupt.

5. Line-level
Cut: "He noted it and moved on." — the prose already moved on; this announces what it should simply do.

Keep: "She had said it twice. He had not needed it twice." — ten words, two characters established, zero wasted motion.
