# Plot Audit — The Skybound Wyrm, Draft 4 — Findings

## Verdict
The draft is structurally sound: across all seven passes the causal spine holds in every act, the shared-reveal contract is never broken, all eight canonical plants and the glamour-exploit/quest-title payoffs land on the page, and motivation stays on-profile. The defects are concentrated and fixable rather than foundational. The top three things to fix first, in order: (1) **the locked-room mechanism contradiction (Blocker, Ch4 vs Ch30)** — Ch4 opens the bolt cleanly with the steward's service-slot lift-key while Ch30, inside the central method payoff, asserts there is no external override and "the bolt had to be cut"; these cannot both be true and the contradiction sits in the solution chain. (2) **The three leaked authoring/meta-commentary notes (two Blockers + one Should-fix, all Ch22–23)** — "The surprise in Ch. 20 was real," "case file Ch. 1–12," and "a thread for Act 3" expose the book's own scaffolding in the prose and must be excised. (3) **The voyage-length / date continuity seam (Should-fix, Ch6/Ch9/Ch12 and Ch38)** — a 3-day trip with an hours-to-port landing clock cannot also span "six days drafting," "the seventh day," and "ten days," and the Ch38 closing entry is mis-dated "October fifth" for a November 17 event. After these, address the unseeded scry-log fairness gap (Ch30), the Sannholt letter Tuesday-vs-Friday timeline contradiction (Ch19/Ch21), and the dangling Esherel saloon/witness plan (Ch12/Ch14).

## Findings by pass

The reader is left holding a setup that the payoff silently discards. (quote: "\"The saloon. The long porthole, if she will sit.\" ... \"I will be at the long porthole this evening.\" [Vellaine] ... ### Pass 4 — System countdown / continuity / locked-room mechanism audit
- **[Blocker]** Ch 30, scene ~middle (Theron walks Sable through the cabin geometry) — contradicts Ch 4 discovery — Direct locked-room MECHANISM contradiction with Ch 4, where the bolt is opened cleanly and is NOT cut. In Ch 4 Calden produces a second steward's key and slides it 'through the narrow brass slot at the height of the bolt and worked his wrist twice... The door swung inward.' That IS an external override (the service slot + lift-key), and the bolt is lifted, not cut. Ch 30's claim ('no external override... had to cut the bolt') invalidates the Ch 4 opening method and is load-bearing in the central solution. State/known-facts.md also records 'Service slot used to draw' the bolt, confirming Ch 4 as canonical. Both cannot be true. (quote: "There is no external override on a Wyrm first-class cabin bolt. The crew used the master-key on the latch to open the door for the discovery, and the bolt had to be drawn from inside before that could be done, which it wasn't, so they had to cut the bolt.")
- **[Should-fix]** Ch 38, scene ~end (Theron's personal-notebook closing entry, Sannholt) — Wrong date on the closing entry. 'October fifth' is the date the Wyrm LANDED at Halverstow (stated in Ch 35 and Ch 36). The Sannholt delivery / closing happens on the SEVENTEENTH OF NOVEMBER (Ch 33, Ch 34, Ch 35 all date the southbound delivery gondola 'the seventeenth of November'). The same Ch 38 paragraph just above even says 'the twenty-eighth' (late October) was when Theron last wrote, and lists 'seven working pages' since — so an 'October fifth' header is internally impossible. The date should be the seventeenth of November. (quote: "> *Closing — Sannholt, October fifth.*")
- **[Should-fix]** Ch 4, two places: the quest-assignment System block vs the chapter's closing narration — Within-chapter countdown conflict of 11 hours. The quest line at discovery sets the clock at 'nineteen hours to Halverstow Port'; the closing narration of the same chapter says 'The Wyrm was thirty hours from Halverstow Port.' Nineteen hours is canonical: it is consistent with the rest of the countdown spine (Ch 5 'eighteen hours' / 'Seventeen hours, fifty-one minutes'; Ch 6 'Sixteen hours, twenty-two minutes'; Ch 11 'Ten hours, seven minutes') and with the three-day-voyage rule. The 'thirty hours' line is the leftover/uncorrected value and contradicts the System spine and a next-morning landing. (quote: "Estimated time on station: nineteen hours to Halverstow Port. [...later in same chapter...] The Wyrm was thirty hours from Halverstow Port")
- **[Minor]** Ch 30, scene ~middle (corridor walk-through) — vs Calden's round schema in Ch 4 / Ch 6 — Adjusted: a one-phrase mislabel, not a re-described round cadence. The numbers ('one bell,' 'three') are consistent with Ch 28's independent description of the same corridor record (nine-T lit at first watch, dark at third); in this world the Nth-watch round occurs at the Nth bell, so 'one bell' = first watch and 'three' = third watch are two DIFFERENT watches across the third NIGHT, not two sub-bell passes inside the third watch. The only genuine defect is the umbrella phrase 'during the third watch,' which should read 'during the third night' (Ch 30 itself correctly uses 'third night' for the same span). Copyedit fix. (quote: "Two pass-by entries from Calden during the third watch. One at one bell of the third watch, one at three. He noted no movement at the cabin twelve door either time.")
- **[Minor]** Ch 30, scene ~middle (porthole line) — vs Ch 4 body-examination — The porthole measurement ('We measured') is asserted but never shown or established. Ch 4's examination records the porthole as 'Sealed at first watch, the brass ring engaged, the seal undisturbed' and never states the frame was measured or that it would not pass an adult; no later scene depicts the measurement. Plausibly done off-page during the on-gondola investigation, so only Minor — and the locked-room logic already rests on the porthole being sealed/undisturbed, making 'would not pass an adult' supplementary reinforcement rather than the load-bearing pillar. (quote: "The latch was not tampered. The frame would not pass an adult. We measured.")
