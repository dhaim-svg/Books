## Flow tic-type quick reference

- **Type A — Dialogue stage-direction one-liners.** Action beats staged as single short sentences opening with a subject pronoun, often standing as their own one-line paragraph; the defect is density (3+ in a scene cost the recoil effect). _Fix:_ merge two beats into one compound, or give the second an adverbial/object-first lead.
- **Type B — Anaphoric "She/He did not" negation chains.** Syntactically identical negated clauses in a run, creating mechanical repetition. _Fix:_ consolidate the negations into a single adverbial unit ("Without looking away or lifting her hand, Sable…") and replace a final pronoun with the name.
- **Type C — Procedural / intentional rhetorical anaphora** (He read / He wrote / He folded). When it echoes legal argumentation or carries psychological weight it is justified. _Exemption — do NOT revise:_ the Ch 30 "The theory explained… / Halsa Renn satisfied…" chains (≈lines 213–221) and the Ch 9 discovery staccato (lines 163–165). (Accidental verbatim echo — e.g. Ch 33 "it's warm" — is the one Type-C case that SHOULD be cut.)
- **Type D — Screenplay beat marker ("A beat.").** A screenwriting stage direction that describes a pause rather than showing it. Found in **3 chapters only: 02, 06, 16.** _Fix:_ replace with a small grounding action, or remove and let the paragraph break carry the pause.
