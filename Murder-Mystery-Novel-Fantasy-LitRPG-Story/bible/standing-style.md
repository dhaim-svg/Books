# The Skybound Wyrm — Standing Style Instructions

*Apply every chapter without exception. Do not repeat these in the chapter itself.*

> **Companion doc:** [brand-voice.md](brand-voice.md) holds the deep prose mechanics, sentence rhythms, dialogue tag economy, and the §8 anti-slop checklist. Both files apply to every chapter; this one is the reveal contract, that one is the voice itself.

---

## POV and reveal contract

POV: Third-person limited, Theron only. Sable's interiority stays off-page;
we read her through what she does and says. This protects the shared-reveal contract.

**Reader and Theron learn together.** The five planted details, the identity swap, and the
killer's identity are discovered at the same moment by both. The shared-reveal contract is
non-negotiable — Sable's interiority stays off-page even in scenes where her insight would
be dramatically satisfying.

## Tense

Past tense throughout.

## Tone

Cozy beach-read on the surface, with one death that has real weight. Banter is welcome.
Grim atmosphere is not. The weight is carried by how characters treat each other, not by
darkness on the page.

## System screens

Sparse. Use them only when they earn the interruption. Format them as indented italic blocks,
not as HUD-style boxes. Theron reacts to them like a graduate who's still pleased the System
exists — not with wonder, not with boredom, but with the mild satisfaction of someone whose
training is paying off.

## Prose

Lean, image-led. Avoid stacked adjectives. No Capitalised Worldbuilding Nouns unless the bible
uses them that way. Dialogue carries character; don't overstuff tags.

## Plant discipline

When a planted detail lands, plant it — don't underline it. The reader passes it without
stopping. **Don't cluster plants in one scene.** Two plants in the same step are fine if they
are in different scenes; two plants in the same scene softens the surprise.

Only after the Act 2 reveal (Step 6) should the planted details come back, in the quiet recall
passage where Sable and Theron sit in the cabin and the details rearrange into a person.
That is the only moment we name what happened.

## What to never do

- Do not summarize the bible into the chapter
- Do not foreshadow the Act 2 reveal directly
- Do not have any character "almost catch" the identity swap
- Do not explain the System to the reader
- Do not give Sable interiority — action and dialogue only
- Do not use HUD-style System boxes
- Do not Capitalise Worldbuilding Nouns not already in the bible
