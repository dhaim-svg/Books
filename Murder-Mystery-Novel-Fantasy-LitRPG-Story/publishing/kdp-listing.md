## Book description / blurb  (≤ 4000 chars; light HTML allowed)

Three options below, low → high on how much they tease. **None spoils the twist.** Pick one (or
mix lines you like and I'll assemble a final). I'd lead with **Option B** for the LitRPG audience.

### Option A — Cozy-forward, setup only (safest)
> Newly licensed and quietly insufferable, Theron Mavik has earned exactly one thing: a rest.
>
> Three days aboard the *Skybound Wyrm* — a grand airship drawn by three harnessed drakes, bound
> across the Brindle Range — should have been the gentlest possible start to his career. Good food,
> good views, nothing to investigate.
>
> Then a first-class passenger is found dead behind a bolted cabin door, and the only investigator
> aboard is a twenty-one-year-old who has never worked a real case in his life.
>
> With a quiet System guiding his notes, a drake-handler who notices what he misses, and a manifest
> full of people who would all rather not be asked, Theron has until the airship lands to work out
> who died, how a locked room stayed locked — and why none of it fits the tidy shape he was trained
> to expect.
>
> *The Skybound Wyrm* is a cozy LitRPG murder mystery: a fair-play puzzle, a warm slow-burn, and a
> world where the magic hums politely in the margins. No gore. No grimdark. Just one clever young man,
> one impossible cabin, and three days of sky.

### Option B — LitRPG + locked-room hook (recommended)
> **A locked cabin. A body that shouldn't be there. And a System that only tells you what you ask.**
>
> Theron Mavik graduated top of his class at the Royal Conservatory — all theory, no practice — and
> his first posting comes with three days off aboard the *Skybound Wyrm*, an airship pulled across the
> mountains by three enormous drakes. He intends to spend it resting.
>
> He spends it standing over a corpse instead.
>
> A passenger is dead behind a bolted door, and Theron is the only investigator on board. His System
> will surface a fact the moment he earns it — a callus, a manifest, an accent that doesn't match the
> face — but it won't tell him what any of it *means*. That part is his. So is the growing certainty
> that the dead man at the center of his neat little case was not, in any way, who the passenger list
> says he was.
>
> Cozy, clever, and scrupulously fair-play, *The Skybound Wyrm* is a LitRPG mystery for readers who
> like their puzzles airtight, their violence offstage, and their detectives in slightly over their
> heads.

### Option C — Voice & character forward (warm slow-burn)
> Theron Mavik knows the deck plan, the lift capacity, and the harness-chain load of every airship in
> the registry. What he doesn't know is people — which is a problem, because somewhere between boarding
> and the Brindle Range, one of them committed murder.
>
> The *Skybound Wyrm* was supposed to be a reward: three days of sky, drakes on the rigging, nothing to
> solve. Instead there's a body behind a locked cabin door, a System that doles out clues only as fast
> as Theron earns them, and a drake-handler named Sable who keeps seeing the things he walks right past.
>
> He sees structure. She sees the man. The case needs both.
>
> A cozy LitRPG mystery with a fair-play heart and a slow-burning warmth — for anyone who's ever loved
> being smart and slowly learned it wasn't enough.
