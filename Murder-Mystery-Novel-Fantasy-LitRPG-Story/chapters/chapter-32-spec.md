## Scenes to hit

1. **Pessel's reply arrives.** Thursday morning at the Halverstow Arms. The post-coach from Sannholt brings a return letter from Pessel — fast turnaround; the city is close. Sable opens it; she reads two of its sentences aloud at the corner table. Pessel confirms what Sable's Tuesday letter asked: yes, the seven years past competence were extra. Yes, Joren taught the apprentice the seating-as-trigger architecture deliberately. Pessel does not name it that way (she says *the late-stage technique*, the *quiet method*) — but the description is unambiguous. The reply closes the master-side gap. Pessel's identification of the apprentice as the *only* one Joren taught the late-stage method to is the load-bearing line.

2. **The subtraction work — the manifest pass.** Theron lays the full Wyrm passenger manifest beside the requirements list. Sable sits with her notebook open and reads the manifest aloud cabin by cabin while Theron checks each name against the six requirement lines. The work is methodical and visible: most cabins fail at line (a) — sustained-contact apprenticeship of seating-phase duration — before any other line needs checking. A handful require two or three lines to clear. The Stallens (cabin ten) clear at line (b). Madam Esherel clears at line (a) but takes a second look. Dr. Vellaine clears at lines (a) and (c). The factor in second-class clears at line (a). One by one the manifest empties. The pass ends with cabin nine-T, Renn, the only name the requirements list does not subtract.

3. **The academy-theory scene — writing the name.** This is the chapter's gravitational centre and must occupy a substantial passage, not a paragraph. Theron sits with the cleared manifest beside the requirements page. He has done the work. The page now has the structural meaning it did not have yesterday: not *converges to one person* but *names one person to the exclusion of all others on the Wyrm*. He opens the requirements page to its second face. He writes, in the unindented hand he uses for working theory now earned, a single line at the foot: *Halsa Renn.* The act of writing the name is rendered specifically — the hand, the pencil, the page. Sable does not name her. She watches him write. The moment is given room: Theron's quiet recognition that the page now says what he has been working toward for four chapters, his refusal to let the recognition tip into triumph, his deliberate naming of what the name does and does not prove. The System pings here — laconic, dry, observational. It notes that the name has been written. It does not celebrate. Theron gives it the small nod.

4. **The known next step.** With the page closed and the name on it, Theron and Sable sit with the question: what happens now. Halsa Renn is in the city. The magistrate has the manifest. The case is not over; it is at the point where the working-theory page has to be carried into a room with the person it names. Theron is clear about that. Sable is clear about that. They do not plan the confrontation in this chapter — that is Ch 33. They name the shape of what tomorrow needs: Davren must be told, the rooms must be arranged, the case book must travel from this table to the magistrate's. The chapter closes with the case book shut on the requirements page, Halsa Renn's name at the foot of it, and Theron and Sable across the table from each other in the kind of silence that follows a piece of work that has finished placing itself.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not foreshadow Halsa's specific motive (the brother / Joren's old contract). That is Spoiler Wall until Ch 33–35. Today is *who*, not *why*.
- Do not let Sable name Halsa. Sable says *one name*, *one cabin*, *the cabin*, *her* — not *Halsa Renn*. Theron writes the name; that is the chapter's beat.
- Do not bury the writing-the-name scene in a paragraph. It is the payoff of four chapters of theoretical work. Give it room — at least half a page of prose around the act itself.
- Do not let Theron be triumphant. The lock is quiet. He has been certain for two days; this morning the certainty earned its right to sit on the page.
- Do not let the System celebrate. One System line, late, dry, observational. It notes that the name was written. It does not congratulate.
- Do not break Sable's POV opacity. Watch her through what she reads, what she writes, what she does not say, the order of her actions. No interiority.
- Do not start the confrontation in this chapter. Ch 33 is the drawing-room build. Today ends at *the work for tomorrow is known*.
- Do not have any character "almost catch" any of the Act 2 reveal content (Vannic alive, identity swap mechanics). That is settled.
- No HUD boxes. One indented italic line for the System.
- No body-language clichés. No "his heart pounded" or "he held his breath." No portentous fragments. Apply §6.
