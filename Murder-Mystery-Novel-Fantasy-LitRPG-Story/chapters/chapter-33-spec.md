This chapter is the **first of three** for Step 11 (Ch 33–35). It does not yet contain the
confrontation itself. Its job is structural: get all three principals — Theron, Sable, Halsa —
into the same fixed, sealed setting that mirrors the Wyrm of Act 1, so that the reasoned reveal
in Ch 34 can take place mid-flight. From `chapters/_chapter-step-mapping.md`:

> Ch 33 | Step 11 — Drawing Room | Confrontation assembled. Passengers gathered.

The narrative mirror to the Wyrm is deliberate. Act 1 opened on Theron boarding a gondola
alone. Act 3 begins its confrontation with Theron boarding a smaller, slower southbound
service with Sable beside him and Halsa under magistral travel-order on the same manifest.

## Scenes to hit

1. **Friday early morning at Davren's office.** Theron delivers the case book. Davren reads
   the requirements page and the manifest with its forty flat lines. He explains, briefly and
   without performance, the legal limit: he cannot order an arrest on structural fit alone. He
   *can* issue a magistral travel-order. There is a southbound morning service to Sannholt
   tomorrow, and Halsa is already booked on it. Davren will instruct her, by clerk before
   nightfall, that her departure is now under magistral accompaniment — that Theron will be on
   the flight, that a clerk of the Sannholt office will receive her at landing, and that the
   purpose is to take a statement *en route*. She is not arrested. She is not free to leave the
   route. She is not told the name at the foot of the page; she is told only that the
   investigator wishes to speak with her under magistral terms during the flight. Davren signs
   the order. Theron carries one copy.

2. **Saturday at first bell — boarding the southbound service.** The mirror to Chapter 1.
   The dock is smaller, the gondola plainer, the handler not Wisp. Sable is already there with
   her bag and a cup of someone's coffee. Theron arrives with the case book in a leather strap.
   They board. They do not see Halsa at the dock; they see her name on the manifest copy in
   Theron's pocket, and they see her bag put aboard by a clerk in Davren's grey-edged coat. The
   lift happens. Halsa does not appear in the dining cabin during the ascent. The chapter ends
   with Theron and Sable at a table in the small forward saloon, the gondola steady at altitude,
   knowing she is two cabins down the passage and that the conversation Davren arranged for
   them to have begins as soon as Theron walks to her door.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not stage the confrontation. Halsa is on the manifest, not yet on the page in dialogue.
  The reveal scene is Ch 34. Holding her off the page in Ch 33 is what gives the boarding its
  weight.
- Do not give Sable interiority. She is in the chapter, fully present, but rendered through
  action and dialogue only.
- Do not let Davren be a courtroom orator. He is a working magistrate of a small town. He
  reads, he asks two clarifying questions, he signs the order. He has been doing this for
  twenty years; the chapter shows that by what he does not need to say.
- Do not let Theron feel triumphant about boarding the gondola. The mirror to Ch 1 is for the
  reader, not for him. He is tired, and the case book is heavy, and the coffee on the dock is
  not as good as the one at the Halverstow Arms.
- Do not put the System in a celebratory register. One quiet line at most, when the manifest
  is officially logged — the System uses *travel under magistral order* as its phrasing, dry.
- Do not foreshadow Halsa's final question, the geographic click of Sable's cottage, or the
  System's quest-title flip. All three belong to later chapters.
- Do not have Halsa "almost" speak to Theron at the dock. She is on the manifest and not on
  the page. That is the chapter's discipline.
- No fanfare for the lift-off. The Wyrm in Ch 1 got the lift; this gondola gets a working
  unsentimental departure.
