## Scenes to hit

1. **Interview with Madam Esherel in her cabin.** Esherel is loud, dramatic, and has a real grievance she's been letting curdle for months. Theron categorizes her at speed: motive, opportunity, no clean alibi, surface-level dislike upgraded by his method to working suspect. He believes he is being fair. He is being structurally narrow. Esherel notices and lampshades it dryly — she's smart enough to see herself being framed by a method, not by malice — and Theron files her honesty as further data without questioning his frame.
2. **A second interview, lighter — Calden in the steward's pantry.** Theron has come for the sealed administrative note (M. Tannin amendment) but ends up walking Calden through the rounds. Calden, properly polite, gives Theron the rounds as a man who has done them, not as a witness being interrogated. Theron categorizes the answers; Calden categorizes Theron. There is a small comic asymmetry: Calden's professional discretion produces more silences than answers, but the silences are themselves data Theron does not yet know how to read.
3. **Brief lounge moment — Sable observed but not engaged.** Theron returns through the lounge between interviews. Sable is there; he does not stop. He has already filed her at the verify-not-pursue tier. He notes that she is, again, looking at the harness chain through the porthole. He does not ask why. He has Esherel-at-1 in his head and the M. Tannin sealed amendment as his next administrative step; "Verrin, S." is not on the working list for this hour.
4. **Return to the cabin. Working note.** Theron writes Esherel up. He writes Calden up. He writes the M. Tannin row up with the small note Calden has now given him (the amendment was made by a passenger who paid the steward's discretion fee on the dock; Calden cannot disclose more without the captain's leave). Theron is pleased with the shape of the afternoon. The reader sees the shape, and sees that it is the wrong shape.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not foreshadow that "Vannic" is not Vannic. No "almost catch" beats.
- Do not let Sable carry interiority. She is in this chapter only in passing — observed from the doorway, not paired with.
- Do not let Theron register his own narrowing. The reader sees it. He does not.
- Do not make Esherel a cartoon. She is dramatic but she is a competent merchant. The cleanest red herring in Act 1 has to be a real person, or the eventual clearing will mean nothing.
- Do not break the cozy tone. The gondola is still flying. The coffee is still good. Theron is still pleased with his method.
- Do not stack System pings. Two at most. They should be dry, not celebratory.
- Do not introduce new plants. Chapter is purely interview-and-narrowing.
- Do not over-explain the M. Tannin sealed amendment. Calden's silence is the scene; the answer can wait.
