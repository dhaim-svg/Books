## Scenes to hit

1. **Evening in the inn's front room.** Theron and Sable take supper after the porch — the room is occupied now, a few working locals, the bar man's wife at the till. Pessel comes through the door briefly to confirm the rooms and to leave word that he'll come by in the morning. Sable asks Pessel, plainly, whether he still has the address of Joren's sister. He does. She tells him she'll come for it tomorrow before she does anything else. Pessel inclines the half-inch and goes back to his work. This is where Sable's promise begins to take concrete shape — Pessel as the address-source.

2. **Theron with the case book, briefly.** Back in his room (or at the porch table after Sable has gone up), Theron opens the case book — not the working book that went to Ottren, but the personal notebook he kept the run in — and writes the closing entry. One short paragraph. Then closes it. He thinks of the next question — the sigil he saw in Halsa's papers at Davren's table — and sets the thought aside. NOT named on the page in any detail; just acknowledged as a thought set down. (Ch 40 territory.)

3. **Morning — breakfast, the harbour, the plan for the day.** Down in the front room, bread and tea. Sable says she'll go to Pessel's after breakfast and ask for the address. Theron asks if she wants company. She says yes for the walk over, no for the conversation. He says all right. They go out. The chapter ends at the door of the inn, before Pessel's — on the working southern-morning light, the harbour, the customs boat at its tie, the pace of a day that has work in it but no urgency.
