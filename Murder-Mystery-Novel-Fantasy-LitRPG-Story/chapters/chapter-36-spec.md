## Scenes to hit

1. **Approach and first bell of the descent.** Cabin two. Theron sitting on the bench where Ch 35 left him, with the case book on the desk and the door of cabin six down the corridor closed. Second bell sounds. He prepares — coat, strap, case book, the small dry ritual he has used every morning of the week. He goes out at half past second bell and meets Sable in the corridor as he met her in Ch 34, by both of them being at their doors. Brief: not banter, working quiet.

2. **The walk down to cabin six at third bell.** Sannholt harbour visible from the corridor porthole — a smaller, southern port, the slip-cranes and customs boats specific. The hum of the drakes changes as the gondola throttles back for set-down. Theron knocks on cabin six; Halsa opens; her bag is on the floor, her coat is on, her hair is pinned. She steps out at his nod. The three of them walk to the disembarkation gangway. Calden of the Sannholt service (not the Wyrm Calden — a different steward, named on the page) sees them through to the gangway and does not perform.

3. **The handover at the magistrate's table.** The Sannholt magistrate (Master Ottren, or named on page — keep it small and not Davren-shaped) is already at the table on the harbour-side warehouse where Davren has arranged the receiving room. The handover is named under the writ: Theron, by Davren's deputation, presents the magistral travel-order, the case book, and Halsa Renn in her own person. Ottren takes possession of all three; he signs the slip; Halsa goes with two harbour constables — not chained, not arrested aboard the gondola, presented at the magistrate's table where the magistrate's office begins. She does not say goodbye and does not need to. Theron does not say goodbye and does not need to. The Friday-note language Halsa quoted in Ch 35 — *the magistrate's table to be ready* — pays off here as a literal table.

4. **The quest completes.** Theron stands at the foot of the table after the slip has been signed and Halsa has gone through the back door of the receiving room. The System pings. The quest closes. The title of the quest is rendered on the screen and the title is **the name it always was**: *Solve the Death of Joren Aldis.* Not "updated to" — was. The System notes, dry, that the quest has been on this title since the first ping in cabin twelve. Theron reads the screen twice. Sable does not see the screen; she sees Theron looking at nothing. He gives the screen the small dry nod he has given the System every morning of the week — and gives this one a half-count longer. **Critical:** this beat must sit visibly. The Step 12 reveal is *the title was always this.* No level-up flavour in this chapter — that's Ch 37. No Sable-feelings-tip — that's Ch 37. No geographic click — that's Ch 39.

5. **After the handover.** Theron and Sable walk out of the receiving room into the Sannholt harbour mid-afternoon. The light is southern, longer, the cranes working, the gondola in its mooring at the visitor slip with the two drakes on their tethered rest. They are not yet doing the things they will do next — that is Ch 37 onward. The chapter closes on a concrete physical detail of the harbour and a small line between them about what is and is not now in front of them. The case book is in Theron's strap. The carved fish is in Sable's pocket. The cabin six door is closed behind them on the gondola; the receiving-room door is closed behind them on the magistrate's side; the case is closed in the only sense Step 12 closes a case, which is not all the senses.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **No theatrical drama at the handover.** It is procedural. No raised voice. No moment where Halsa nearly bolts. No constable having to put hands on her. The Friday note set this out and the morning has already been spent. The book has paid for the quiet.
- **No interiority for Sable.** When Sable walks beside Theron, when she sees him at the foot of the magistrate's table, when she walks out of the receiving room — we have her actions and a small number of her words. No window into what watching this costs her.
- **No "they did it" Theron interior celebration.** Theron has been at the foot of his own table for a week now. He is not pleased; he is finishing. The chapter is not LitRPG triumph-coded. The System line lands dry.
- **No System fanfare around the title flip.** The reveal is the reveal because it is small and trocken. No "the System has been watching you all along." No "you have grown." One italic block, two or three lines at most, plus Theron's half-count nod. The discovery is in **the wording of the title line** itself — *Solve the Death of Joren Aldis. Quest closed.* — and a separate line that names, dry and laconic, that this has been the title from the first ping.
- **No second System line in this chapter** beyond the closing one. No "Insight +1" beat. No timer line. The chapter has one System intervention; it is the load-bearing one. Anything else would dilute it.
- **No "the System knew" rendered in narration.** The reader concludes it from the line; the prose does not say it. Theron registers it without naming it to himself in lecture form. Sable does not see the screen. The shared reveal is the reader-and-Theron contract.
- **No level-up flavour text or stat readout.** The chapter ends before the *seeing the unseen* flavour line, which belongs to Ch 37.
- **No "we did it" / "thank you" beat between Theron and Sable.** That kind of conversation is Ch 37 ground. Here they say small working sentences and walk out.
- **No reference to Sable's onward travel or her cottage.** That is Ch 38 / 39 ground. She is in Sannholt; she has a thing to do; the chapter does not name it.
- **No new plot information** about Joren's network, Roven, Toll, or the Sannholt address paper from Ch 35. The paper is on a desk in cabin six and the chapter does not return to it; that thread is for Davren's clerk later.
- **No clusters.** The handover is one continuous beat. The System title-flip is one continuous beat. The harbour out-walk is one continuous beat. Three discrete beats, each given its own weight.

## Length and shape

Target: **~2,000 words** (range 1,800–2,500). Five scenes as listed above. The chapter is shorter than the Step-11 chapters around it; the procedural shape of the handover and the small architecture of the System reveal do not need three and a half thousand words to carry.

The chapter ends on a concrete detail of the harbour — a working specific (a customs boat, a gull on a piling, the gondola in its mooring with two drakes at rest, the light on the slip — pick one and let it carry) — and a small line between Theron and Sable that does not name what they have done. The case book in Theron's strap; the carved fish in Sable's pocket; the case closed in the sense Step 12 closes it.
