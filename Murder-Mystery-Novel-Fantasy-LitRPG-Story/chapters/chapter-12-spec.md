## Scenes to hit

1. **Late morning, the gallery — Theron closes the last gap in the page.** Theron wakes early, takes the slate down to the captain's gallery, and finds the bow's long forward window already at the second-watch coffee. He sits with the slate flat on his knee and works at the one column he had not finished yesterday: *opportunity, late window.* Calden's confirmation from Ch 9 had put Esherel and the corridor sightline in one breath; the harness-chain note from Ch 11 sits in the cabin-twelve margin unattended. Theron does not connect them. He works the late window — the three-minute gap Calden gave him — and writes a small clean *opportunity (late)* under Esherel's column. He does not underline it. He sets the pencil parallel. He waits to see whether the page reads as finished. It does not, yet. There is one piece he wants to confirm before the underline: he wants to know whether Esherel's cabin door has the same sightline-kink to cabin twelve that he and Sable established for the corridor on the seventh day. He decides he will check at the open of the lunch service, when she is at her table. He is unhurried about it. The page is patient.

2. **Midday, the corridor outside cabin twelve — the last sightline.** Theron walks the deck-three corridor at the open of the lunch service, when Esherel is at her table and her cabin is empty. He does not ask for entry; he does not need it. He stands at the cabin's threshold from the corridor and looks down it. The sightline from her door to cabin twelve carries the same half-step kink he and Sable had walked on the seventh day. He had built the case-page on the corridor's geometry from Esherel's table, not from her cabin. Now he has the second sightline. He files it. The lunch service is just beginning to send up its small clean noises through the deck plates. He goes up to the dining room with a small new tick to add to the column under *opportunity (late)*. He is not floating. He is unhurried in the way a man is unhurried when he has finished a thing properly.

3. **Lunch, the dining room — banter with Vellaine; the last category.** Theron takes his chair across from Vellaine. The folio is closed at Esherel's table again — closed at the third bell, on yesterday's pattern. Vellaine does not look up. They share a small clean exchange about the lunch pot's blend and about the Henners' daughter's egg today, which she has eaten with less ceremony than yesterday. Vellaine, without looking up, asks Theron whether he has finished the page. He says he is one tick from finishing it. Vellaine turns a page in her book. She says: *you should speak to her with the page finished, then.* He agrees that he should. He says he intends to speak to her after the supper. She turns another page. She does not say *be careful with the cleanest column* a second time, because the line landed in Ch 10 and the second saying would underline it. She says, instead: *I'll be at the long porthole this evening if you want a witness.* Theron registers it as an offer, and as one of the small adult kindnesses he is not used to receiving outside the academy. He accepts it. He drinks the coffee, which is, on second pass, very good. The room has been the room since the third morning. Esherel does not turn a quarter today. She finishes her bread. She rises and leaves through the forward arch.

4. **Afternoon, the gallery — the underline.** Theron returns to the gallery with the slate after lunch. He sits on the bench at the end that is not the brass. He works the page for fifteen minutes — not for new evidence, but for the careful look the page wants before a decision he has been carrying for two days. The two windows on a single line. The corridor in pencil. The sightline-kink from Esherel's cabin door, ticked. Demeanor under stress, two ticks. The harness-chain *carry-frame* note in the cabin-twelve margin, sitting patient and unconnected — he leaves it where it is. He writes *opportunity (late)* under Esherel's column and underlines it once with the parallel hand he had begun to favour. He looks at the two underlines on the page, *demeanor* and *opportunity*, and they read as a column that has finished closing. He caps the pencil. He lays it parallel to the slate's top edge at the angle Sable lays hers. One dry System line lands; he reads it twice and does not flinch. The decision is made: he will speak to Esherel after the supper this evening, in the long-porthole corner of the saloon, with Vellaine present at the next table as the agreed witness. He does not write the decision on the page. He files it under his own attention. He is comfortable.

5. **Early supper, the saloon with Sable — cozy at peak.** Sable comes up to the gallery again at the open of the bronzed hour. She has not been told the decision. She does not ask. They go down to the saloon together — a slightly easier walk than yesterday's, the pace settling between them — and take the corner table at the long porthole. The cook has done a different soup tonight, a thicker one with the small white pieces and a green oil on top. The lemon biscuits come as six. Sable moves two onto Theron's side without comment. He notices the count. He registers the cook's correction without naming it. The banter runs slightly warmer than yesterday — a small running joke about the bolt on the port runner that has *decided it did not* loosen, a small running joke about Petal's two-breath slot to Gravel, a small running joke about Vellaine being paid in symposia. They are not flirting; they are easier with each other. At some point Theron tells Sable, plainly and without preamble, that he intends to speak to Esherel after the supper. Sable looks at him for a long beat. She says only: *the long porthole?* He says yes. She says: *I will be at the corner.* He says he had not asked. She says she had not waited to be asked. They finish the meal. The conversation does not move into the case again. They talk about the cottage on the coast briefly — Sable's errand, the inheritance, the great-aunt she had barely known — and Theron does not press. He has not pressed at any meal in the run. She is grateful for it in the way that does not get named. The Wyrm carries its low constant through the floor.

6. **Closing — the slate set down, Theron confident, the gondola on the long approach.** Theron walks the slate up to his cabin at the close of the bronzed hour. He sets it on the writing desk parallel to the desk's far edge as he has the past two nights. He looks at the page once. The column is closed. The underline on *opportunity* is fresh. He laid the pencil parallel. He does not return to the slate; he does not open it again. The cabin smells faintly of his father's dispensary oil. The drakes are on the long approach. The Wyrm is doing its slow careful thing toward Halverstow Port through a sky that has the gold of late afternoon already thinning toward the bronze. Down through the floor, the harness's note carries the same low constant it has carried since boarding, and underneath it, when he listens for it, the secondary note from yesterday is still there. He does not connect it. He moves his thumb off the graduation pin. He has the small clean sensation of a thing finished properly — the column, the page, the ten days of work — and an evening still ahead of him to do the talking part. He puts on a clean shirt for the supper. He goes down. The chapter ends with the door of his cabin closing softly behind him on the slate and the lamp and the desk, and the corridor outside still warm with the bronzed light, and the page on the desk completing itself in his absence by being done. Comfortable. No dark cloud. The gondola flies.
