## Scenes to hit

1. **Fourth-tier re-examination.** Theron works through the third-class passenger list with fresh categories. Not "who had motive against Lord Vannic?" but "who had motive to stop Joren Aldis?" or "who knew the swap?" This is where the dismissed suspects walk back in — not as confirmed leads, but as people whose dismissal was based on the wrong frame. The chapter does not name Halsa Renn as a suspect; that's Act 3. But the structural logic should allow the reader to see that someone Theron filed as fourth-tier may have more relevance than his first pass showed.

2. **Calden gives Sable the third-class manifest.** Before Sable boards Friday's coach, Calden finds her (or she finds him) and hands over the third-class manifest with his crew notes: which passengers spoke to staff, which stayed completely silent (only cabin acknowledgment), which seemed unusual in ways he filed at the time and didn't know how to place. This is not romantic — it's practical. Calden is a competent steward; he reads the room; he gives her the professional tool because she's the one doing the work that follows. The System notes it dry: she is not Theron's assistant. She is parallel.

3. **Sable's pre-departure exchanges.** Theron and Sable have a brief working exchange — they compare what they each have going into their respective threads. Theron has the partial cargo trace, the Roven ghost, the Fell thread still open. Sable has the Sannholt address, the two-year context for Joren's expected visit, Calden's manifest notes, and the decision to find Pessel before any port-office letter arrives. The exchange is practical, not sentimental. The chapter closes with Sable's bag packed and the coach departure confirmed for seventh bell Friday.
