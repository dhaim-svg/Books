## Scenes to hit

1. **Aftermath of the Calden interview, debrief at the alcove.** Theron meets Sable as agreed in Ch 8. He summarizes what Calden gave him — start-minute and end-minute of the twelve, the M. Tannin amendment context partially confirmed, the half-second slowing again. They compare what each got. Brief, working, warm.
2. **The pivot — Sable raises Esherel.** They sit somewhere quieter (reading alcove at the stern, or the second-class bay window — pick what's not freshly used; use the alcove if Sable wants light at her shoulder for the slate). Theron has Esherel at rank 1 in his ledger. Sable says, in passing: *I don't think Esherel sent it.* Theron asks her to expand. She reads the letter back — not the words, the *shape* of it: someone who spent four rewrites on the third paragraph and stopped mid-fourth was composing the not-replying, not preparing to kill the man. The letter is for Esherel, not for Vannic. A person who is in that state is not a person who is hours from murder.
3. **Theron, politely, dismisses it.** He hears her, he respects her, he files it as *Sable's read on Esherel's temperament* — and he sets it next to motive (41,500), opportunity (alibi window does not cover the relevant period), and demeanor under questioning (candid). The letter's shape is a feeling about a letter. He doesn't say feelings are not evidence — he is more graceful than that. He says he's keeping Esherel where she is on the page until something moves her off it, and that this is the correct discipline. Sable lets him. The banter is the carrier: light, mutually amused, no one wounded.
4. **Closing beat.** They split for the evening. The dismissal is on the page. The narrator does not underline that Theron was wrong. He will be wrong by Ch 13; the reader's job here is to register the disagreement without being told what to do with it.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not let Theron "almost catch" anything related to the Act 2 swap.
- Do not have Sable explain herself in interiority — only action and dialogue.
- Do not have the narrator confirm that Sable is right and Theron is wrong. The reader will know later.
- Do not soften the dismissal into half-agreement. Theron *dismisses*. Politely. Fully. He keeps Esherel at rank 1.
- Do not make the disagreement combative. They are warm; they are competent; they have different methods.
- Do not introduce a new plant, a new suspect, or a new piece of evidence. This chapter is about how two people *who already have the same evidence* read it differently.
- Do not have Calden say anything load-bearing about the M. Tannin amendment beyond what the captain authorized. The cabin three thread keeps its hold — pay it off later.
- No System ping that comments on the disagreement directly. The System is laconic; if it pings here it observes something tangential. Cap at one screen, ideally none.
