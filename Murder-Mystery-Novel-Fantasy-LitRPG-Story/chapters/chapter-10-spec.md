## Scenes to hit

1. **Morning, Theron's cabin / first-class corridor — recall of the corridor step-aside.** Theron wakes after sleeping on Calden. He works the page. Sometime mid-scene, walking the corridor with the slate to find Sable or just to move, he passes the spot where, the morning of the boarding, "Vannic" had stepped aside in the corridor for an elderly passenger — no audience, no performance, no produced gesture. Theron, now thinking about Vannic in the past tense, finds the memory surface unbidden because his attention is on what the man did and did not do. He registers it. He files it: *not relevant, a noble's small idle courtesy.* Moves on. **Plant 1 lands here. ONCE. No reprise.**
2. **The dining room — Esherel makes a scene.** Mid-morning. Esherel and the wine steward again, but this time it's a different ledger irregularity (a manifest discrepancy on a southern crate count — small, real, the kind of thing she is right about). She raises her voice. She does not shout, but she escalates: she stands; she names the steward's previous error from Ch 2; she names her own dispute with Vannic in the open dining room, where Vellaine, the Henners, and a second-class passenger up to fetch coffee can all hear. The scene is brief, loud, and ugly in the small clean way Esherel is ugly — surgical, righteous, unflinching. The room reads her as a woman in extremis. Theron reads it as a woman whose grip is slipping. He files it under *demeanor under stress.*
3. **The chartroom or the captain's gallery — Theron walks his case against Esherel out loud (to himself, mostly, slate in hand).** He builds the column. Motive on the page. Opportunity on the page. Demeanor under stress now on the page (the scene). The early window from Ch 9 still uncovered. He is closer to *opportunity confirmed* than he was yesterday. He underlines *demeanor* in his head and the System pings briefly and dryly. He is not yet writing the conclusion. He is preparing the page that would carry the conclusion.
4. **The lounge or stern alcove, late afternoon — Sable's offhand line.** Theron finds Sable. He does not lead with Esherel. He shows her where the page sits. They talk a little about the morning scene, sideways. At some point — over a cup of something, in the natural drift of conversation about how the room reacted — Sable says, half-shrug: *Vannic wasn't as bad as I expected.* The line lands in the air. Theron registers the words. He does not ask why. He does not file it under the case. He files it under *small talk between two people who have been thinking about a man.* He lets it pass. The scene continues for one short beat after the line, so that the line is not the closing beat (it is buried, properly buried, in a longer breath). **Plant 2 lands here. ONCE. No reprise.**
5. **Closing — Theron back at the slate.** End of day. He sits with the page. Esherel underlined again. He feels close. He does not write the conclusion. He closes the slate. He is comfortable. The reader leaves the chapter with him at peak confidence in the wrong direction.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **Do not let Plant 1 and Plant 2 share a scene.** Strict rule. The chapter is the test of the don't-cluster rule.
- **Do not have Theron almost-catch the swap.** When the corridor step-aside surfaces in his memory, his attention is on Vannic-as-suspect-class (idle noble vs. striving noble), not on Vannic-as-person. He files; he moves.
- **Do not give Sable interiority** when she delivers the offhand line. The line lands; he files; the scene moves. The narrator does not signal that the line mattered.
- **Do not let Theron flinch at Sable's line.** No "something about it caught him." No "he almost asked." File and move. The whole point of the plant is the price of his not being curious enough.
- **Do not make Esherel's outburst a confession-shaped scene.** She is not raving. She is right about a specific ledger error and loud about it. The reader should be able to hold both: she is genuinely wronged, she is genuinely under strain, and a reasonable observer might read her as the killer here. The chapter earns Theron's false confidence by making Esherel a person whose pressure is visible.
- **Do not cluster the textural beats** — only one cozy set-piece beyond the dining-room scene (a Wisp moment or a drake observation in passing, no more).
- **Do not narrate the false conclusion as false.** No "he was wrong about her." No "if only he had known." The chapter sits with Theron's certainty and lets the reader sit there with him.
- **Do not have the System ping more than twice.** Both pings should be dry, observational, slightly cooler than Theron's mood. The System sees the page he is building; the System does not see the thing he is missing.
- **Do not use the niece-softness.** That plant is Ch 11.
