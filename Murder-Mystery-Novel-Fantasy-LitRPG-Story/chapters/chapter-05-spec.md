## Scenes to hit

1. **The captain's chartroom.** Theron meets Captain Joriss (offstage to date) for the formal notification — gives a clean version of the locked-cabin facts, gets the captain's procedural cooperation (corridor closed, manifest delivered, port wireless held back to the last hour). Establishes the clock: nineteen hours has become roughly eighteen. Sable not present; this is Theron in his official capacity.

2. **Theron's cabin, working the manifest.** First sustained academy-mode beat. He categorises every name in first and second class against possible motive against "Vannic": rivals, jilted parties, debt-collectors, merchants with disputes. Esherel surfaces first and loudest. He builds a working list — not conclusions, categories. The System gives one dry observation. Sable present briefly: she brings the manifest from Calden (a class signal — Calden gave it to her, not Theron), exchanges six words about it, leaves. Not yet partnered; she is *also working*, in her own register.

3. **The first-class lounge by midday.** The news has spread. Esherel is loud in her displeasure — not because Vannic is dead, because the gondola is being inconvenienced. Vellaine is quiet and watching. The Henners family is present; a second-class passenger or two has drifted up to see. Theron observes the room and adds notes to his categories. Sable is at the corner table again with her property papers, working through them — present, occasionally looking up, doing nothing investigative but not absent either. He registers her. He does not yet approach her.

4. **Close on the cabin door.** Theron passes cabin twelve. Calden at his post. The cabin is sealed, locked, master off the ring. He stands a moment. He has a working list. He believes the method. He believes the method is the right one. (The reader sees the method engaged; the book knows it is the wrong shape for this case.)

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do NOT have Sable say anything that points at the swap or at "Vannic" being not-Vannic. She is present, she observes, she has her own quiet business. No "almost catch" moment.
- Do NOT give Sable interiority. We read her through what she does and says, period.
- Do NOT plant the accent slip, the second kindness, or any other Joren-detail. Those have their assigned chapters.
- Do NOT have Theron consider whether the corpse is really Vannic. He has zero reason to. The body wore Vannic's clothes, slept in Vannic's bed, carried Vannic's correspondence and signet. The reader, with him, takes the identity as given.
- Do NOT use a status-screen recap at the end of the chapter. No bulleted suspect list reproduced on the page. The list is referenced as something Theron is building, not displayed.
- Do NOT let Theron be infallible. There must be at least one beat where his method is *slightly* over-confident — a category he commits to too quickly, a small thing he files wrong. He doesn't see it. The narrative does.
- Do NOT explain the Investigator class. The method is shown, not described.
- Do NOT cluster the suspect names into a single info-dump paragraph. Distribute them across scenes; let the reader meet motive through observation, not exposition.
- Do NOT have Esherel be cartoonishly loud. She is brittle and inconvenienced; she is also a competent woman whose feelings about Vannic were business, not personal. The red-herring volume is earned, not performed.
