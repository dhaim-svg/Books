## Scenes to hit

1. **Manifest study.** Afternoon, cabin. Theron pulls the full first-class passenger manifest from the case slate and works it like a case file even though there is no case. Names, regions, booking dates, the one post-departure amendment from Ch 2. He builds preliminary categories on each passenger, catches himself doing it, sets the slate aside, picks it up again, sets it aside more deliberately. Self-discipline as active labor.
2. **Wisp at the rigging house.** Late afternoon, stern equipment deck. Theron wanders out for air and finds Wisp working on the harness lines. The three drakes are visible off the bow on their long tether-flight. Wisp talks about the drakes as colleagues; Theron asks one technical question and gets twenty minutes of education. The scene shows the working life of the gondola without exposition. No suspect work happens.
3. **Overnight passage begins.** Dinner is quieter than breakfast. Theron eats with Vellaine again, briefly. "Vannic" comes to dinner and leaves early. The dining room thins out by second watch. Theron goes back to his cabin, lights the lamp, opens the slate one more time, closes it, gets into bed. The Wyrm's harness creak settles into night-rhythm. He sleeps with the feeling that the holiday is going to be all right.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not foreshadow the Act 2 reveal. No "Vannic" suspicious behavior. He is barely on the page; when he is, his choices are mundane (eats and leaves).
- Do not let Theron *almost* notice the swap. He notices the amendment row on the manifest in Ch 2 and continues to choose not to pursue it. He does not theorize what the amendment is.
- Do not let the manifest scene become an investigation. Theron is exercising restraint, not gathering data. He builds and discards working categories on each passenger. The scene's emotional content is *not investigating*, not the contents of the manifest.
- Do not introduce Sable as a named POV-relevant character. She's still off-page or, at most, glimpsed without identification. The woman in the brown coat from Ch 2 is *not* Sable. (Sable enters properly later.)
- Do not over-describe the drakes. Wisp scene is texture, not worldbuilding lecture. One technical question, one warm answer.
- Do not let the System ping more than twice. Two is the cap for this chapter — the manifest line and one closing line — and both must earn their interruption.
- Do not close on a thematic gloss. End on a concrete detail — the lamp, the harness creak, the slate face-down — and let the day end honestly.
- Do not write "and then he slept" / montage closure. Compress with white-line breaks, not narrator summary.
