Ch. 25 closes Step 8 and Act 2. Sable departs for Sannholt; Theron stays in Halverstow. The investigation has split into two parallel threads. The closing image of Act 2 is the carved fish going to Sannholt with Sable — Theron gives it to her to carry to Joren's family.

## Scenes to hit

1. **The cargo trace result arrives.** The magistrate's clerk returns the full search result on the cargo job reference numbers from Fell's interview. The registered agent for the Mirren west-harbour storage firm is identified — a name that may or may not connect to Roven (partially suggestive, not conclusive). This advances the Halverstow investigation thread into Act 3 territory and gives Theron a specific next step to follow after Sable's departure.

2. **The departure morning.** Sable is at the coach clearing house at seventh bell. Calden is present (the gondola is still docked; he is completing the passenger discharge paperwork). Esherel may be present to depart herself — her portion of the story is complete and she is leaving Halverstow on her own business. The morning is brisk and specific. The coach is real: other passengers, luggage loaded, driver checking the manifest. Sable is a person departing on a coach, not a symbolic figure.

3. **Theron gives Sable the carved fish.** Before the coach pulls away, Theron gives Sable the small wooden fish to carry to Joren's family in Sannholt. He has carried it since Ch. 4 — logged it as an odd personal effect, carried it through the recall, held it in Ch. 16, carried it ever since. He gives it to her without ceremony; she takes it. The fish belongs with the family, not in a magistrate's effects locker. This is the closing image of Act 2: the fish is on its way to where it was meant to go, and Sable is the one carrying it. The coach pulls out. Theron watches. The investigation continues, split across two cities, with the right victim's name in both notebooks.

## Plants landing in this chapter

**Landing:** The carved fish surfaces one more time — not as a recalled plant, but as an object passed from Theron to Sable for delivery to the family. This is the final deployment of the fish, distinct from the Ch. 16 recall: there it was held; here it is given.

**NOT landing:** The other four plants do not recur as beats. The fish passing hands is the act of the object, not a recall passage.

## Continuity

Ch. 24 ends with Sable's bag packed, the coach confirmed for seventh bell Friday, Calden's manifest in her possession, and Theron's working threads identified for after her departure. The case is split: Theron in Halverstow (Roven, Fell, cargo trace), Sable in Sannholt (Pessel, Joren's two-year purpose, the family). Tone: brisk and forward.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- No Sable interiority.
- The fish handover is not melodramatic. Theron gives it without ceremony; Sable takes it. The weight is in the object, not in the dialogue around it.
- Do not resolve the cargo trace. The result is partial and points toward Act 3.
- Do not name Halsa Renn as a suspect.
- Do not make the departure sentimental. Two investigators, two threads, one case. The relationship is warm and professional; the parting is not a romantic farewell.
- This chapter CLOSES Act 2. Do not begin Act 3 investigations.
