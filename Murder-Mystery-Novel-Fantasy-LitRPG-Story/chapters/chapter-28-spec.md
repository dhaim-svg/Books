## Scenes to hit

1. **Halverstow, Monday morning, magistrate's small office** — Theron walks the working column over to the Halverstow magistrate (the one who has been carrying the case at this end since Toll). They sit at the side table. Theron lays out the column dispassionately: cabin nine-T, Kolach placement, Sannholt destination, Pessel apprentice line via Sable. The magistrate listens, asks two pointed questions, and is professionally satisfied that the column belongs in front of them. He is *not* yet professionally satisfied that it is a case to bring forward. The exchange surfaces what is missing: a method. There is no description of how a death in cabin twelve was caused by a passenger in cabin nine-T who never entered cabin twelve.

2. **Halverstow, Monday late morning, the Arms — partial Mirren transcript arrives** — The post-coach brings an early-release fragment of the Kolach transcript: the magistrate at Mirren's end has wired the personal-referral source ahead of the full transcript because it was the one piece Theron specifically asked to expedite. The referral source is named: it is Joren Aldis himself, eleven years ago, when Halsa was new in the trade. The note is two sentences. Joren put her into the network. The placement in the current year was the renewal of a credential he had vouched for when she was twenty. Theron sits with this — a tragic complication, not a clearing — and puts it into the column without commentary.

3. **Sannholt, Monday afternoon, via Sable's third letter** — Sable, before leaving Sannholt, walks the market quarter and finds the Copper Finch, where Pessel's apprentice used to be hosted. She speaks to the proprietor — a guarded conversation, no name given, only confirmation that a young woman of the description came three or four times a year and stayed in the back room and stopped coming two years ago without any quarrel, simply stopped. The proprietor remembers she always travelled south after Sannholt, never north. Sable writes this to Theron and is on the evening coach home. The letter does not arrive in time for this chapter — only the news that she is travelling and will be in Halverstow by Wednesday.

4. **Halverstow, Monday evening, the Arms — the *how* problem** — Theron sits with the column and the new placement-source line and the magistrate's two questions. He has the *who*. He is sure of the *who*. The method is what is missing. A passenger in cabin nine-T did not enter cabin twelve on the night Joren died. Calden's steward notes say so. The Wyrm's corridor watch says so. The door was bolted from inside. He cannot account for how. He writes the question on a fresh page: *Method — unknown.* He does not solve it tonight. He closes the case book on the question.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not name Halsa Renn as the killer on the page. *Working suspect* only. The magistrate confirms the column belongs in front of him, not that it constitutes a case to bring forward.
- Do not have Theron solve the method. The chapter ends with *Method — unknown.* That is the point.
- Do not foreshadow the scryproof glamour exploit. Theron has not yet remembered his thesis material. That is Ch 29 (Step 10).
- Do not have the Joren-referred-Halsa-into-the-network line become an exoneration. It is a complication: Joren put her in the trade; he had no idea her motive against him would form. Theron registers this with care and does not editorialise.
- Do not give Sable interiority. Her Monday afternoon is narrated only by what she sends in a letter (and only the dispatch is mentioned this chapter, not the contents — letter not yet arrived).
- Do not have the magistrate be obstructive or doubtful. He is professional. The two questions he asks are the right questions, which is why they sting.
- Do not stack System pings — max 4, target 2.
- Do not Foreshadow Halsa's brother / the contract that killed him. The motive direction is *eight years ago* still off-page.
- Do not collapse the chapter into a single contemplative scene; the four-beat structure holds.
