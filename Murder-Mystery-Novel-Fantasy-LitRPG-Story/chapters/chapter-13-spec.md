## Formula coverage

**Step 5 — The Floor Drops — Vannic Is Alive.** The shared reveal. Theron and Sable find "Petor Vann" together in third class; the reader learns at the same beat. He is Lord Petric Vannic, alive, hiding from a forced betrothal to a duke's daughter twice his age. The corpse is not his.

## Scenes to hit

1. **Sable's discovery in the third-class quarters.** She has turned up something — a name in the third-class register, a passenger who fits, a piece of texture Calden didn't mean to give away. Show only her action and what she reports to Theron, no interiority.

2. **The shared-reveal beat in third class.** Theron and Sable enter a compartment or alcove together and see "Petor Vann." Recognition lands on the page for Theron at the same moment it lands for the reader. This beat is the chapter's centerpiece: the *shared reveal* — reader and Theron learn simultaneously, one line, no forewarning.

3. **Immediate aftermath in a private space.** Petric's panicked confession of the betrothal, the swap arrangement, his humiliation at being found. Theron's reorientation: the corpse is not Vannic. Sable is present — she observes, she may ask one question — but the reader does not enter her thoughts.

## Plants landing in this chapter

**Landing:**
- None of the five Joren-identity plants (calluses, accent, carved fish, kindnesses, niece-softness) become investigative or focal in this chapter. They remain textural background only, still attributed to "the dead man" or "the lord." This chapter is about the living imposter, not the dead illusionist.

**NOT landing this chapter:**

- Accent slip (coastal lilt) — textural only
- Carved fish (small wooden object) — textural only

## Continuity

Ch. 12 ends with Theron comfortable, the page finished, going down to supper in his good shirt to speak to Esherel at the long porthole after the meal. Sable is waiting at her corner table, unhurried, having told him plainly she would be there. The gondola is on its long approach to Halverstow Port through a bronzed sky. Tone: cozy at absolute peak, Theron unhurried and confident in the wrong direction.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not give Sable interiority at the discovery moment or anywhere else. Show her action, her words, her questions — not her feelings or reasoning.
- Do not include any "almost caught the swap" beat before the reveal. The reader learns the truth when Theron does; no dramatic irony ahead of him.
- Do not let the reader learn about the reveal before Theron does, even by one sentence. The shared-gut-punch contract is absolute.
- Do not name Joren Aldis in this chapter. The dead man is still "the dead man," "the lord," or "the body." Joren gets named on the page in Ch. 16, Sable forcing it.
- Do not foreshadow the Step 6 recall passage or hint at the retroactive rearrangement of the five planted details.
- Do not darken the cozy register. This is surprise and embarrassment, not horror. Petric is ridiculous and caught; Theron is reoriented and moving to the next question; Sable is pragmatic.
- Do not resolve any of the five planted details or signal their future significance.

## Length and shape

Target: **~2,500 words** (range 1,800–3,500). Opens on a specific action — Sable's report, a corridor, a small piece of information — not on weather or time-of-day. Closes on a concrete detail or a latent question Theron carries forward (the corpse's identity now the urgent question, Petric's panic, a fact that needs checking), not on thematic gloss.

### Scene 2 or Scene 3 addition — Vannic bridge beats

Within Scene 2 (shared-reveal beat) or Scene 3 (confession aftermath), insert 1–2 sentences of narrator observation or Theron's perception that bridge the Ch. 1 composed-mage register to Vannic's current state.

**What the bridge accomplishes:** A cold reader coming from Ch. 1's imposing, hyper-observant aristocrat needs a line that explains why he is now sleep-deprived and inarticulate. The external reader found the humanization successful; the bridge makes it legible to analytical readers without altering the destination.

**Authorized execution options (choose one or combine):**
- Theron notices Vannic's physical state: three days in third class, wrong sleeping rhythms, the composed register he used on the dock now visibly effortful to maintain.
- A brief narrator observation: the man before him is recognisably the same person as the callused mage on the dock — but stripped of the controlled setting that made that register sustainable.
- Vannic himself acknowledges the shift implicitly: a sentence where the composed register surfaces briefly (he catches himself, straightens, uses a precise phrase) before collapsing again, showing the register is still there but no longer the default.

**Hard constraints:**
- 1–2 sentences maximum. This is a bridge, not an explanation.
- Do not make the bridge a lecture or an internal monologue by Vannic.
- Do not alter the reveal mechanics or the confession beat — only the surrounding register description.
- Theron's narration maintains close-third; no omniscient explanation.
