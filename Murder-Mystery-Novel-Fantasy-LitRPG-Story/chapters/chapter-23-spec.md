## Formula coverage

**Step 8 — Inversion: Suspect Pool Flips (opening chapter).** "Everyone Theron cleared walks out. Everyone he dismissed walks back in. Esherel becomes a source... The case restarts with less time on the clock and a less experienced detective for the work that actually matters."

Ch. 23 executes the first movement: those Theron cleared (first-class suspects — Esherel, Vellaine, second-class passengers) recede from the working circle. His investigative categories — built for noble rivalry, not for a working-mage illusionist with a professional network — were the wrong shape for the case.

## Scenes to hit

1. **Theron's method-breaking realization.** Sitting with what he knows: Roven placed Joren, Fell was on the passage for cargo-side reasons, the network was tracking Joren from inside the gondola. Theron reviews how he cleared his first-class suspects and sees what was wrong: he asked "who had motive against Lord Vannic?" when the question should have been "who had reason to stop Joren Aldis?" All the first-class cases collapse — they were never valid frames for this murder. He doesn't blame himself; the method was correct for the case he thought he had. The method was built for the wrong case.

2. **The cargo numbers partial result.** Theron follows the cargo job reference numbers Fell gave in the Ch. 21 interview through the magistrate's clerk. The result: the two spring customs-assist jobs trace to a specific cargo-route account connected to a Mirren west-harbour storage firm. The firm's registered agent is not Toll — it's a name Theron hasn't seen before (possibly connected to Roven, possibly a dead end requiring more work in Act 3). The trace is partial: useful, not conclusive.

3. **Esherel as a source.** Esherel approaches Theron (at the harbor, at the Arms, or on the magistrate's front step) and offers one more observation about Joren — something she held because she didn't know it mattered. Something she saw in a private moment: Joren alone at a table, or in a corridor, reaching into his coat pocket for something small and looking at it for a moment before putting it away. She didn't know what it was. She knows now (she's been told it was a fish carved by his niece). She tells Theron because someone is going to tell Joren's family, and they should know that he had it with him at the end and still reached for it. Esherel is not sentimental; she is precise. This is her as a source, not as a witness.
