## Scenes to hit

1. **Theron finds the captain; Sable is already with Calden in the pantry passage.** Theron came down to the steward's station to ask Joriss's leave to push Calden on the twelve-minute absence; he arrives to find Sable in the doorway and Calden mid-sentence, telling her something he stopped telling Theron yesterday. Theron pauses outside the frame; the conversation continues without him; the class signal is the entire moment. He files it without yet knowing what to do with it.
2. **Theron and Sable take a shared meal at the small window table in the dining room while the lounge is still working through the captain's noon address.** Quiet hour. They compare what each of them knows. Theron walks the structure; Sable walks the people. The asymmetry surfaces clean — he sees the manifest, she sees the man. Inside this conversation, Sable mentions a fragment of "Vannic's" speech at the first night's dinner: a vowel that didn't sit right, a word he reached for and bent, said again correctly the second time. She names it as a thing she noticed and chose not to mention because correcting a lord at dinner is rude. Theron files it under *small talk that isn't useful*, asks a follow-up that isn't the right one, and moves on. The plant lands and leaves.
3. **The two of them walk the corridor outside cabin twelve together — first joint look at the scene.** Theron walks the geometry; Sable walks the doorways. She points out where Calden's body would have been in the third-watch round and where it would have been on the fourth — the steward's path read as a man's path through a corridor, not a manifest's notation of times. Theron sees a thing he didn't quite see before. They part on a small concrete agreement: she'll see what Calden says next; he'll go back to the M. Tannin door.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- No "almost catch." Sable does not nearly say something more revealing than she says. The plant is one beat and it passes.
- No foreshadowing the Act 2 reveal. Theron does not "have a feeling" about the accent. The System does not ping on the accent. Calden does not look meaningfully at anyone.
- No Sable interiority. We do not know what she thinks; we only know what she does and says.
- Calden's opening-to-Sable is a moment of behavior, not a paragraph of explanation. No internal monologue from Theron about class systems. He sees it; he files it; the prose moves.
- No romance escalation. They are now working partners and they recognize each other as competent. That is the warm beat. Nothing about hands, nothing about glances meeting.
- No second plant in this chapter. The accent slip is the only one. (Verify §8.)
- No HUD-style System box. Max one or two System interventions, both as indented italic lines, both earned.
