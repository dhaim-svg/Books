## Scenes to hit

1. **Theron at cabin two with the strap.** A short opening beat: he sits with the case book on the desk, he runs through what he will and will not say, he leaves the cabin. The Wyrm-bone hum through the floor. The slate corridor.
2. **The walk to cabin six.** Down the corridor, past Sable's door — she joins him at her threshold, no theatre, just the agreed shape of the morning. They go together. Knock.
3. **Halsa opens.** The door, the small cabin, the choice of where to sit. The southbound gondola is a third the length of the Wyrm; the cabin is a third the size. The book is laid on the small writing desk. Sable goes to the cabin wall, in the chair against it, not above the page but near it — the same posture she took in Davren's office.
4. **The laying-out.** Theron walks Halsa through the case: the Kolach channel referral, the Pessel letters, the seating-phase trigger architecture, the manifest with its forty flat lines. He uses the language of the case book. He does not name the requirements page (that page stays in Davren's case-room). He asks no question. He puts the work down.
5. **Halsa listens.** She does not interrupt. She does not deny. She has a particular stillness. When she speaks she says one or two short things — about the work, about Pessel, about the seating phase, about the page — that make it plain she is not contesting it. The chapter ends on the moment of plainness: Halsa has not denied, the conversation is not finished, and Theron is sitting on the other side of the desk waiting for whatever she means to say next.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **No theatrical reveal.** No "I know what you did." Theron lays it out as case-book language. He does not accuse. He presents the work and waits.
- **No "almost catches herself."** Halsa is not surprised. The sealed note from Davren told her enough. She has had a night. The chapter renders the morning of someone who has had a night.
- **No interiority for Halsa.** We are still close-third on Theron. Halsa is read through what she does, what she says, what she does not say. The chapter is the *experience of laying it out to someone who is letting you*.
- **No interiority for Sable.** Sable's role here is the same as in Davren's office: at the wall, present, named on the order, not above the page but near it. She speaks at most one or two short sentences, and only if the scene requires.
- **No System fanfare.** At most one or two System interventions — laconic, observing what the room is doing or what Theron is not saying, not narrating the confrontation.
- **No spoilers from Ch 35.** The question about Joren's name belongs to Ch 35. The brother — if it surfaces here — surfaces through Halsa's choice and only partially. The full motive is hers to bring.
- **No purple language.** No "the silence stretched." No "her eyes held his." The chapter is built in the same recursive, antiphonal, case-book voice as Ch 32 and Ch 33.
- **No power-fantasy beats for Theron.** He is not enjoying this. He is doing it because the work led here and because Davren built the morning so that he would be the one to do it.
- **Do not close on summation.** Close on a concrete image, or a held line, or one dry System line.
