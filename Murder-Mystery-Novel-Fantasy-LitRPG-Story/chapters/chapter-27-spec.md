He works through the question of why his training didn't catch her: the noble-rivalry template, the motive-shape he was taught to expect, the assumption-by-class that filed her as fourth-tier. 2. **Sannholt, Sunday morning, Pessel's front room** — Sable arrives at the named hour. Tea, a small front room, two women who do not need to perform anything for each other. Pessel speaks first about Joren's Sannholt errand in general terms — there is a child, there is money set aside, he had been working toward coming home. Then Pessel names the apprentice line: yes, there was a young woman Joren trained, years ago, who used to come through and sit in this room. Pessel does not say her name. Sable does not ask. The conversation is two people who knew him from different sides, taking turns.

3. **Halverstow, Sunday afternoon, Theron writes back** — Sable's second letter arrives by return coach (she sent it from the inn at midday). Theron reads it. He writes the apprenticeship-line confirmation into the working column and adds the structural finding from his morning's work: his training pattern recognised noble-rivalry, and that pattern excluded the entire shape of person Halsa was. He writes the short reply to Sable. The chapter closes on a quiet System line and a settled cup of coffee.
