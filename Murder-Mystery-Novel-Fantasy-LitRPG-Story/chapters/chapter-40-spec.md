## Scenes to hit

1. **Monday and Tuesday morning at the inn — Davren's report.** Procedural-bookkeeping opening. Theron writes the closing report for Davren over Monday and into Tuesday first bell — at the table in the front room, in his own small closing hand. The report goes to the chandler's letter-pouch for the Tuesday coach back to Halverstow. The procedural register is the rhythm of the chapter's first beat: dated entries, the warden's stipulated format, the inn at its working pace. Sable's departure has already happened (Monday morning, off-page in compression — referenced in one line). The first scene closes on Theron sealing the report and handing it across the bar to the barman to give to the chandler's boy.

2. **Pessel's bundle — the sigil.** Tuesday late morning. Pessel comes to the inn personally — not the boy — with a small leather bundle under his arm: *the rest of what Joren had left with me to hold for the family.* Mostly innocuous: a small clothbound book, a receipt or two, a folded letter (the one referenced in Ch 39, already accounted for). Pessel sets the bundle on the table for Theron because Sable is south and the family is north and the chandler does not propose to forward paperwork to a grieving sister without it being read by someone who has a class for the reading of it. Theron opens it at the table. Most of what is there belongs to the family. One thing does not — a single folded slip, smaller than the others, with a contract fragment in a clerkly hand and a stamped device at the corner: a sigil Theron does not recognise. Two lines of contract text — a fee schedule and a delivery clause for something not named, signed with an initial only, dated three months before the Wyrm run. The device at the corner is the same shape as the one he registered on the sheet from Halsa's papers at Davren's table — registered but not pursued — and which had not been Halsa's mark either. He notices that. He notices it in the small inner movement he has practiced not making a thing of since the warehouse. He does not say anything to Pessel about it. He asks Pessel one question (does Pessel know what this slip is) and Pessel does not. Pessel cannot help. Theron thanks him. Pessel goes. Theron sets the slip aside — not in the family's bundle, not in his own working notebook, but in the back pocket of his personal notebook, where things he had not been asked to investigate had been collecting for a week. The scene closes on him closing the notebook.

3. **The Tuesday coach — booking and boarding.** Theron walks to the coach office across the lane and books the early-afternoon Tuesday coach. He picks a small concrete destination — **Mirren Vale** — because it is two days inland by coach, has a hot-spring inn an academy fellow once told him about over a long lunch in fourth year, and he has been told twice by Warden Davren (and once by his thesis advisor before her, and once by his mother in the same letter that mentioned his father's lumbago) that the spring water is the part of the country people went to specifically because it had nothing on it. He pays the fare. He walks back to the inn. The barman serves him bread and the cold midday cider, knowing now what cider Theron orders without asking, and the gulls and the carters and the boy with the bucket are at their working pace, and the last hour at the inn is the last hour at the inn.

4. **Closing image — boarding the coach.** Theron settles his strap on the coach's outside bench (an outside seat by choice, the way of a man whose three days in a sealed cabin have left him preferring open air for a while). The notebook is in the strap. The slip with the unknown sigil is in the back pocket of the notebook. The coach driver counts heads. The horses settle. The harbour bell at first hour of the afternoon strikes once. The coach moves. End on a small concrete detail at coach pace: not a horizon line, not a "looked toward the road" gesture. Something quiet and specific — the coach driver's elbow finding the rest on the door rail; the lane sliding back at the slow working pace of departure; the painted board over the chandlery still saying *Pessel & Son* in white over older white as it goes past. Last sentence concrete, warm, without commentary. Theron is on holiday and the notebook in his strap has an entry in it that is not finished and is not for finishing today.

## Plants landing in this chapter

**Landing:**

- **The sigil / contract fragment in Joren's effects (Book-2 thread).** Discrete one-time plant. Theron notices it. He asks Pessel; Pessel cannot help. He files it. He does not chase it. The mark matches the one he had already registered on the sheet from Halsa's papers — registered but not pursued — which links Halsa's network and Joren's outside contracts to the same unidentified party. The link is *visible to Theron* (one half-sentence of recognition: *the same corner-mark as the sheet at Davren's table*) but it is not investigated, not explained, not resolved. This is the page-one of the next book and the last page of this one.

**NOT landing this chapter:**

- No further reveals about Halsa, Joren, the Vannic case, or the niece. Those are closed.
- No further appearance of Sable (she leaves on Monday, before the chapter opens — present in the chapter only as a single referenced line of remembered Sunday-evening parting and one written line in Theron's report). No farewell scene. The farewell happened in Ch 39 in the only form it would take in this book.
- No System level-up. No quest-board scene. At most one System line, dry, observing what Theron has set aside — and possibly zero. The chapter is in the procedural register of a man writing a report and catching a coach.
- No System screen explaining the sigil. The System does not know. Or if it does, it is not telling.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **No sentimental farewell flashback.** Sable's exit was complete in Ch 39. Do not reopen it. One line in the report ("Miss Verrin south on the Monday coach, per her own arrangement") and possibly one passing inner registration — that the chair across the table at breakfast was empty — is the maximum. No more.
- **No "and the story isn't over" gesture.** The sigil is the thread. Do not gloss it. Do not write a paragraph about its implications. Do not have Theron think *whoever this is, I will find them.* The thread is unresolved because Theron does not pursue it on the page. He notices it. He sets it down. The reader feels the weight of that on their own.
- **No system level-up.** Ch 37 paid the level-up. Doing it again would cheapen it.
- **No "he was changed by it all" gloss.** The closing image is concrete. No interiority summing up the book. No "and he understood, now, that…" sentence. Cut.
- **No mirroring of Ch 1 announced in the prose.** The mirror is structural. Do not have Theron think about boarding the gondola at first bell. The reader's memory does the work. The prose does not.
- **No romantic tension in absentia.** No paragraph about Sable. One line, if any. The relationship is at the right point and any further pressure on it in Ch 40 would tilt the book sentimental at the last moment.
- **No "the case wasn't really over" line.** The case is over. The new thread is a separate question. The voice respects the closing of one thing before the opening of another.
- **No cliffhanger.** The chapter ends with Theron in a coach on his way to a hot-spring inn. He is on holiday. The holiday is not metaphorical.

## Length and shape

Target: **~2,000 words** (range 1,500–2,500). Four scenes as listed above, lean and procedural, with the rhythm of late chapters (Ch 37–39): short declarative sentences, "all right" / "half-inch" cadence, working-southern register. The chapter closes the book; it does not orate.
