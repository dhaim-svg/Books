## Scenes to hit

1. **Pessel's front room (off-page for Theron).** Theron walks Sable to the door at first bell (already done at end of Ch 38). The Pessel meeting happens with Sable inside. Theron is in the inn waiting. Brief opening: him at the inn, the morning continuing, the second cup of tea, the quiet held.
2. **Sable returns to the inn.** She comes back into the front room with the address. She has two papers in her hand — Pessel's address slip for Joren's family, and her own inheritance papers (the great-aunt's cottage, the property work she was doing in the passenger lounge on Ch 2). She sets them down on the table together. Theron, with his Investigator's habit of reading documents at angles, registers that they share something — the place-name on the address Pessel gave her is the same coastal stretch as the address on her inheritance papers. He does not say it. He notes it.
3. **The geographic click on the page.** Sable says one or two lines that put the recognition on the page without naming it as recognition. ("I know this stretch." Or: "That's three miles from the cottage." Or: "Pessel's hand says it the same way the deed does.") Theron registers her stillness — the same stillness she had three doors down the corridor in Ch 1 when "Vannic" thanked Calden by name — without naming it as the same. He waits. She does not say anything more for a beat. Then she says she will go. She will tell the family. Her cottage is in the region, which she is not making a thing of, but the fact of it sits on the table between the two papers.
4. **Onward.** Sable folds both papers together and puts them in her notebook. She tells Theron her plan — she will leave for the cottage in a day or two, and then she will go to Joren's family from there. The carved fish is still in her inside coat pocket. The chapter closes on the small specific gesture of her putting the two papers — Pessel's slip and the deed — together into the same fold of the notebook, and Theron registering that the gesture is itself the answer to a question he had not known to ask.

## Plants landing in this chapter

The geographic click is the structural payoff this chapter delivers. The five planted details from Act 1 have already been recalled at Ch 16–17. The "hidden geography" from `bible/character-profiles.md` and `bible/ending-twist.md` (Sable's cottage is in Joren's home coastal region) lands here.

**Landing:**
- **Geographic click.** Sable recognises the address Pessel gives her as a place within her cottage's region. The reader sees it on the page — through Sable's action (her stillness, her line about knowing the stretch, the two papers laid side by side), through Theron's observation (he sees what she does, he reads the documents at an investigator's angle, he registers her quiet). Theron does not need to fully name it; the reader recognises the geometry. **Per the spoiler guardrail and standing-style rule, Sable is not given interiority — the click is conveyed by what she does and says, observed by Theron.**

**NOT landing this chapter:**
- The unfamiliar device/mark from Halsa's papers at Davren's table (held for Ch 40 as the book-two thread).
- No fresh System ping (or at most one minimal dry line — Investigator registering the geography without naming it).
- No emotional spike, no romantic beat, no almost-moment. The book-two romance will be staged in subsequent material; here the recognition is professional and geographical.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do **not** give Sable interiority. The geographic click runs through her action and dialogue and Theron's observation, never through her thoughts.
- Do **not** have Theron "almost catch" the deeper book-two implication — Sable's cottage being in Joren's region, his niece living there, the network reaching south, Halsa's old territory. The reader sees the geography click; Theron sees the geography click; neither extrapolates further.
- Do **not** make this a dramatic confrontation or a romantic beat. It is a quiet recognition on a working morning in a working inn.
- Do **not** introduce the unfamiliar device/mark from Halsa's papers — that's Ch 40.
- Do **not** make Sable announce the geographic coincidence as a discovery. She is a person who reads people; she would not turn her own click into theatre.
- Do **not** use HUD-style System boxes. Maximum one dry indented italic line, if any.
- Do **not** name the region with a fully invented coastal-toponym that has not appeared in the bible. Either let the address read in a deliberately quiet way (a stretch, a coast road, a parish name) or invent one named-place anchor that is internally consistent with the southern coastal Halveth setting and the existing toponymy (Sannholt, Halverstow, Mirren).
- Do **not** cluster the two recognitions in the same beat. The setting-down of the address and the setting-down of the deed should be sequential, with at least a small physical or conversational beat between them, so the click lands by accumulation rather than as a stacked reveal.
