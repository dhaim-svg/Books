## Scenes to hit

1. **The Kolach result arrives — faster than expected.** The Halverstow magistrate's clerk returns mid-morning with a wire-message summary from the Mirren counterpart. Kolach was interviewed under the letter of introduction; he broke quickly because his vouching irregularity was already on the magistrate's books. Marbeth's identity papers were filed through a referral channel Kolach operated as a side service — discreet credential placement for clients who paid cash and provided no professional references. The wire summary lists the names of credential placements filed through that channel in the past year. Theron reads the list. One name reads: *Renn, Halsa. Booking credential placement, third class, Skybound Wyrm, twenty-third departure of the current year.*

2. **Theron pulls the third-class manifest and the Renn revisit notes.** He works the cross-reference at the table in the Arms. Halsa Renn boarded under her real name — that part was honest. What was not honest was the booking credential: cash bookings on the Wyrm required a reference, and her reference had been placed through Kolach's channel — the same channel that had issued Marbeth's identity papers and that had handled the Vannic-Joren contract paperwork. Halsa was in Joren's professional network. Not noble-rivalry. Not Vannic's world. Joren's.

3. **The first letter from Sable arrives at the Arms by return post-coach.** She writes from a small inn in Sannholt at end of first day. The letter is brief and factual: Pessel has agreed to speak in the morning; Sable has confirmed Pessel knew Joren as a working colleague over many years; Pessel's relationship to Joren was a business one with personal weight. One Sable observation that matters: Pessel mentioned, in passing, that Joren had a former apprentice. Female. Coastal, like him. Pessel did not give a name and Sable did not press. The detail is in the letter because Sable thought Theron should have it.

4. **Theron writes back. The shape of the case is now visible.** He drafts a return letter to be sent on the next post-coach. He names what the Halverstow thread has turned up: the Renn credential placement through Kolach's channel. He does not call her a suspect; he calls her a name that should be in front of them both. He closes the case book on the day with the new column open: *Renn, Halsa. Cabin nine-T. Wyrm passenger. Booking credential through Kolach (Mirren). Sannholt destination. Former apprentice of Joren Aldis — to be confirmed.*

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **Do not identify Halsa Renn as the killer.** She comes into focus as a name in Joren's network. The chapter ends with her in the new suspect column, not with Theron certain.
- **Do not foreshadow her motive.** The brother, the contract, the eight-year arc — none of that surfaces. Halsa is a name and a network connection. No "almost catch" moments.
- **Do not have Sable on-page.** Her independent investigation in Sannholt reaches Theron through her letter. Her voice on the page is in her written words. No POV-break.
- **Do not let the Kolach result solve the case.** It surfaces a name. The name is consistent with the Sannholt destination and the late lamp, but it is not yet evidence of murder. Theron treats it as a thread, not a verdict.
- **Do not cluster reveals.** The Kolach list and Sable's letter are different scenes, separated by Theron's working time at the table. The two pieces touch the same name but arrive through different doors.
- **Do not announce Act 3.** No "and so the real case began" gloss. The chapter opens on the clerk arriving and closes on a written line in the case book.
- **No HUD boxes.** System interventions sparing — two at most. Indented italic blocks only.
