## Scenes to hit

1. **The gondola underway — Theron's cabin, morning.** The Wyrm is aloft and running. Texture: the sway, the sound of the drakes through the hull-bone, Calden doing his rounds. Theron is awake early because of course he is. He does something that reveals his Investigator class in a low-stakes way — cataloguing what he can observe from the corridor or using the System for a trivial note. The System obliges with something dry and unremarkable. He accepts it with mild self-satisfaction and goes to find breakfast.

2. **First-class dining room.** Theron circulates and is slightly insufferable about it. He meets Dr. Vellaine properly — she recognizes his class pin and greets him by name, which pleases him more than he'd admit. Madam Esherel is audible from the other end of the room, already unhappy about something. "Vannic" is present but not interactive — visible across the room, eating alone. Theron reads the room the way he was trained to: a quick catalogue of everyone present, a small deduction spoken aloud (to no one; he's that person), the habit of assigning roles before he's learned names. This is the showing-off beat, and the prose should let him be right without letting him be admirable about it.

3. **The woman in the brown coat.** A quiet, separate beat — Theron notices her again, near a writing table in the lounge or reading a letter in the corridor outside the dining room with the particular expression of someone doing paperwork they didn't travel to do. No exchange. He files her as: own errand, no interest in the other passengers, probably dull. The reader is allowed to suspect he's wrong. He moves on.

### Scene 3 addition — Sable seed beat

After the existing Scene 3 beat (Theron files the woman in the brown coat as "own errand, probably dull"), add approximately one paragraph before the chapter closes.

**What the beat accomplishes:** Theron notices something slightly off about Sable — a small detail that doesn't fit the property-papers persona she projects. He files it without understanding it. This gives the reader (and Theron) a prior observation to draw on when the partnership crystallises in Ch. 7.

**Authorized execution options (choose one):**
- She asks Theron something about the second-class manifest process — odd for a first-class passenger — and he notices she already knows the answer.
- She makes a remark that shows she has clocked the lounge's occupants with a precision that surprises him.
- He notices she is reading the second-class passenger list in a way that is slightly too attentive to be curious.

**Hard constraints:**
- No Sable interiority. She is read only through action and dialogue.
- No disclosure of her professional identity or background.
- Theron files the observation; he does not act on it or pursue it.
- The beat must fit within the existing lounge scene — it is not a new scene, it is the last paragraph of Scene 3 or a brief close-of-chapter beat.
- Word count addition: ~100–150 words maximum.
