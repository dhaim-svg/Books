## Scenes to hit

1. **Late morning, outer promenade — dragon-flight banter with Wisp, magical clue.** Theron is out on the starboard promenade walking off the morning page. Wisp is at the rail with a tension gauge again. Banter about the drakes' formation — Mottled has stopped rushing the downbeat, Petal is showing a new line on the upswing, Gravel is sulking about the harness rebalance. Theron, slate in pocket, lets Wisp's working monologue carry him. **The magical clue beat:** standing at the brass rail in the slipstream, Theron notices something about the harness chain's tone that he had not noticed for nine days — a thin secondary hum, a fraction off the main note, the kind of resonance the academy textbook on long-contact sustained casting would have called a *carry-frame*. He files it under the cabin-twelve question (no wound, no powder, no bottle: a sealed cabin and a man dead) because the textbook's example for the carry-frame had been *the unattended sustained illusion field*, which he has been thinking around without naming for two days. He does not name it now either. He registers it. He does not show Wisp the slate. Theron is showing off, but only to himself, and the System does not chime. The cozy set-piece carries the scene; the magical clue is its background hum.
2. **Midday, dining room — Esherel escalates, now at Theron.** Lunch service. Theron has taken a seat across from Vellaine again, two pastries and the cabin-twelve page lying face-down beside his plate. Esherel is at her table with the folio closed, which is new. She does not stand. She turns one quarter on her chair to face him and addresses him over six feet of room in a voice that is not raised but is, like yesterday, the same instrument tuned a half-step higher. The substance: she has noticed his case slate is out at every meal and that his line of sight at her own table has been a degree longer than at any other. She names the thing in front of the Henners and Vellaine. She does not accuse him of anything; she asks him whether he has any questions he would like to put to her in front of witnesses, because she would prefer to answer them with the room as a record. The room does not know what to do with this. Vellaine looks up from her book for the first time that morning. Theron — to his slight surprise — handles it. He answers without flinching, without grandstanding, without lying. He says he is working the page and will speak to her when the page is ready. She accepts the answer at face value and turns back to her plate. The scene is brief, public, and ugly in the small clean Esherel way. Theron files it under *demeanor under stress, escalating.* He is now closer to *opportunity confirmed* than he was at breakfast, and is also aware, in a small honest beat, that he has not quite registered why this fact pleases him.
3. **Afternoon, captain's gallery — Theron showing off (to himself, and to the page).** The case-build continues. He sits with the slate in the captain's gallery, where the long forward window gives him the bow view and the drakes in formation against the wide pale sky. He works the column. Motive on the page. Opportunity in two windows. Demeanor under stress now in two scenes. He draws, this time, the corridor geometry with a finer hand than yesterday; he sketches the harness-chain resonance note in the margin under cabin twelve in three small lines, as a possibility he has not yet decided how to use. He is enjoying the page. He is, in a part of his attention he is choosing not to audit, performing the page for himself — the careful hand, the parallel pencil, the small clean ticks. One System line, dry, glancing the blind spot but not naming it. Theron reads it twice. He does not flinch. He is comfortable.
4. **Late afternoon, dining room or saloon — the meal Theron remembers.** Sable comes and finds Theron at the gallery near the close of the bronzed hour and they walk down to the saloon together for the early supper. The saloon is the small forward room on deck three where the second-watch passengers take a light meal before the evening service; Theron and Sable have eaten there together once or twice in the run. Over the meal — bread, a soup, the small clean lemon biscuits Sable likes — they fall into the kind of conversation that has been theirs for two days now, the half-banter half-working talk. At some point the talk reaches the cook, who came out last night to mention the bread schedule; and at some point it reaches the second-class child in the blue coat who is no longer airsick; and at some point — by the small drift talk has when two people have been at the same table for half an hour — Theron remembers, plainly, a moment from the third dinner of the run, where "Vannic" had been at his wall-facing table behind them. **The niece-softness lands here.** Theron remembers the words; he speaks the remembered sentence aloud once, to Sable, in the past tense, because he is thinking about the man whose body has been in cabin twelve and the small social details of a man at a meal have been on his mind. The sentence Theron remembers is one line. He repeats it once. Sable, who had been at the next table over that night and who had registered the tone shift, has a small still moment — a half-second pause with her bread halfway to her plate, a glance at the porthole and back — that the narrator does not name. The conversation moves on inside two sentences. Theron files the words under *small social texture of a man whose body is in cabin twelve.* He does not file it under the case. He does not return to it. **PLANT LANDS ONCE — no reprise.**
5. **Closing — Theron back at the slate, peak confidence intact.** End of day. He goes up to his cabin with the slate, sets it on the writing desk, looks at the page. Esherel at the top. The two windows. The harness-chain note in the cabin-twelve margin. The four-crate scene and the lunch escalation noted under *demeanor under stress* in two clean ticks. He still does not write the underline — Sable's discipline from yesterday holds — but he is closer to it than he was. He closes the slate. He is comfortable. The reader leaves the chapter with him at peak confidence in the wrong direction, with the niece-softness already five hours behind him.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **Do not foreshadow the Act 2 reveal.** The carry-frame harness-chain note is a clue that will resolve in Step 10; in this chapter it must read as a textbook reference Theron is filing, not as a hint at the killer's method. He names *carry-frame*; he does not name *scryproof glamour* and does not approach the connection.
- **Do not cluster.** The chapter has one plant only (niece-softness). Wisp's banter, the magical clue, Esherel's lunch escalation, and the meal conversation are all texture — none of them is a second plant. The plant lands in Scene 4 alone.
- **Do not let Theron almost-catch the swap.** When he remembers Vannic's sentence about the niece, his attention is on *the man whose body is in cabin twelve had a moment of softness at the table on the third night.* He does not wonder why. He does not file it under the case. The whole point is the price of his not asking.
- **Do not give Sable interiority** at the plant. Her reaction is one physical beat (a pause with the bread, a glance to the window and back, the half-quarter on the cup). The narrator does not signal that the line mattered to her. She does not say *huh* or *interesting* or *say it again.* She lets it land. The plant must be the most fleeting of the seven; this is the moment to discipline the prose hardest.
- **Do not have Esherel break.** Her lunch escalation against Theron is precise, public, and unraised. She is not raving. She is right that he is building a case on her, and she is choosing to put it in front of witnesses. The reader should still be able to hold both readings of her — pressured guilty woman, pressured innocent woman.
- **Do not narrate Theron's confidence as false.** No "he was wrong." No "if only." The reader sits in his comfort. The next two chapters will do what they do.
- **Do not let Theron show off to Sable.** When he is in the gallery with the page, he is showing off to himself. Sable does not arrive until late afternoon. The page is a private pleasure, lightly exposed.
- **Do not have the System ping more than twice.** Both pings should be the dry observational register from Ch 9–10. The System sees the page; the System does not see the niece.
- **Do not write *niece*** the second time. The word appears once, in the remembered sentence Theron repeats aloud. After that, it is gone for the rest of the chapter.
- **Do not have anyone use Joren's name.** That is locked for Step 6 (Ch 14).
- **Do not produce a HUD-style System box.** Indented italics only.
