## Scenes to hit

1. **Sable arrives at the Arms at last bell.** She is tired from the coach. The host knows her by name; she takes the room next to Theron's and comes down for food, not for the case yet. The first ten minutes are warmth — bread, the white-bean soup the host saved, the small physical business of a traveller arriving — and Theron lets her eat before he opens the case book. The thesis abstract is laid out face down on the table between them. She sees it without touching it.
2. **Theron walks Sable through the four lines on the method page.** He reads them aloud once, then offers the abstract. She reads it twice, asks two questions to make sure she has the technical shape (the seating phase; the signature compatibility; the activation-from-distance), and writes nothing. The asymmetry of §5.2 stands: he over-explains the theory, she names the working consequence in one sentence.
3. **They test the theory against cabin twelve.** Theron draws the cabin geometry on the back of a sheet — door, bolt, porthole, the corridor pass. Sable adds the corridor sheet from her notes. They walk it together: the door bolted from inside; the porthole latched; the steward's pass-by record; the lamp-uncertain note. The theory does not require Halsa to enter cabin twelve. The theory does not require a wound. The theory does not require anyone to have passed the bolt. The geometry stops being an impossibility; it becomes a precondition the exploit was built for.
4. **They test the theory against the scry record.** Theron has the gondola's standing scry log from his Wyrm intake. Standing-detection registered no cast magic in cabin twelve during the third night. The exploit's defining property is that, once seated, it does not register as cast magic — it registers as ambient field, if it registers at all. The negative on the scry log stops being exculpatory and becomes confirmatory. This is the second click. Sable says the line that lands the chapter: *only an illusionist, or someone trained alongside one, would know to look for a method that does not show up on a scry.* He writes it as the fifth line on the method page.
5. **The structural-passing finding, the absence of proof.** Theron and Sable sit with what they have. The theory explains the cabin. The theory explains the scry. The theory requires three things of the killer: training next to an illusionist for long enough to satisfy the seating phase; signature compatibility; access to the trigger. Halsa Renn satisfies all three. So would, in principle, any other apprentice Joren had trained. Theron does not know whether Joren had only ever trained one. They write the missing piece on the page in plain ink: *exclusivity to Renn — not yet established.* End on the page with the five lines on it, the cabin geometry sketch beside it, and the scry log on top, and the quiet of a working table at the Arms after second watch with the kitchen closing down for the night.

## Continuity

Ch 29 tail recap: Tuesday morning at the Halverstow Arms, Sable's road-letter arrived ahead of her with three answers from the Copper Finch and Hesh's unprompted line *did work that needed quiet rooms.* Theron pulled out the slate-bound notebook he had not opened since the Conservatory and reread his final-year thesis abstract and the defence script, then wrote four working-theory lines on the method page: seating phase satisfied by eleven-year apprenticeship, signature compatibility between two illusionists, activation does not require caster near target, trigger known only to caster. He recognised the corollary he had not stated at twenty-one — that a class of exploit that does not register to current scry-detection will arrive looking like a sealed cabin and an unwounded body. ## What NOT to do this chapter

- Do not have either of them name Halsa's motive. The brother is a Spoiler Wall fact for Step 11.
- Do not let the theory become certainty on the page. The chapter ends on *structural passing*, not on proof.
- Do not give Sable interiority. Her thinking is on the page as questions and one-sentence consequences only. Theron watches her think.
- Do not turn the cabin-geometry test into a chalkboard lecture. It is two people at a table looking at a sketch.
- Do not write a System fanfare for the click. The System, if it speaks at all, is laconic.
- Do not foreshadow Step 11's drawing-room confrontation. The chapter ends on the page, not on a plan.
- Do not let either of them be triumphant. Joren is still dead. The theory holding is a finding, not a celebration.
- Do not allow Sable's coach-trip warmth to slip into saccharine. Per §5.3: warmth is concrete and slightly inconvenient. She is tired; she eats; she takes off her travelling coat; she does not perform affection.
- Do not cluster two technical clicks in one beat. The cabin-geometry click and the scry click each get their own beat in their own scene.
