## Scenes to hit

1. **Harbour walk into Sannholt.** Pick up from Ch 36's close: Theron and Sable have just walked off the working pier at the slow pace of two people who have finished one thing and not yet begun the next. They go inland a short way along the harbour wall to the harbour-side inn Theron and Sable have agreed (without agreeing) to stop at. The afternoon light is southern and long; the working slip is behind them; the gulls and the customs boats are at their normal afternoon work. Small concrete detail of the inn's outer wall — a board with the day's stew, a hitched cart, a cat on a step. They take a table on the inn's back porch which looks out over the harbour, not the street. They order food. They have not eaten since the dawn loaf on the gondola. They are tired in the way a week makes a person tired. No banter — small, working sentences. Sable carries the carved fish in her coat pocket still; Theron carries the strap with the case book emptied of what Ottren has now.

2. **The level-up lands at the table.** Theron is sitting with his hands around a mug. The food has not arrived yet. The System pings — quietly, without ceremony, the way it has done all week. The screen renders an *indented italic block* that names the level-up: Investigator level X, and the flavour line *seeing the unseen* (or close to it — dry, laconic, naming what he learned in this case, which was the type of suspect his training had filtered out). One additional dry observation from the System about what the line means in this particular case — that the suspect his training taught him to look for had the wrong shape, and that the suspect actually responsible had the shape he now knows how to see. Two or three short italic lines, not four. No stat sheet. No allocation. He reads it once. He reads it a second time. His face is doing what it does when he reads a thing the System has put in front of him that is more accurate about him than he was. The chapter's load-bearing System intervention sits here, and only here, and it earns its line.

3. **Sable sees his face.** This is the scene that carries the chapter. Theron is reading the screen; Sable is sitting across from him at the table; she sees his face. She does not see the screen — the contract holds — but she sees the man who is reading the screen, and what she sees is the small specific change in his expression when he reads the flavour line a second time. She has been reading him for a week. She does not, this time, look away. She is allowed to look. Theron does not see her looking, because he is reading. The scene is short — six to ten lines, no more — and is rendered entirely in what she does and what she says, never in what she thinks. **The required move:** she does or says one small thing that the reader receives as the shift in her, without the prose telling the reader what it is. Possibilities (pick one, render concrete): she sets her hand on the table where his hand is not; she says something brief about the flavour line he has not told her about; she does not say anything and just lets him finish reading. Whatever the choice — it is concrete, small, and the reader feels the tip without being told there has been a tip.

4. **Brief exchange about what the screen said — without Sable seeing it.** Theron tells her, in two or three sentences and only when asked, that the System levelled the class with a line about seeing the unseen. He does not lecture. He does not analyse. He names the line dry. Sable answers in one short sentence that is hers — Sable-shape, brief and right — and the exchange is over. The level-up is rendered, the contract is held, and we have not given Sable interiority to do it.

5. **The end of the chapter.** They eat. The food arrives — a stew, bread, something warm. They have a small working talk about what is in front of them, not the night and not the week. They are in Sannholt; the harbour is doing its work; the gondola is in its mooring at the visitor slip behind them. They do not name what is next — that is Ch 38–40 ground. The chapter closes on a concrete physical detail of the harbour-side inn (the steam off the stew, the light on the table, the cat on the step, the harbour gull on a piling, the bread torn at) and one short line between them that does not name anything large. Do not foreshadow the cottage. Do not foreshadow the geographic click. Do not foreshadow the book-two thread. End in the small working pace of two people who have eaten a meal together at the end of a week.

## Plants landing in this chapter

This chapter does not plant new mystery details. The Step-12 reveal that lands here is the **level-up flavour line** and the **reversal of the reveal contract**.

**Landing:**
- **The level-up flavour line *seeing the unseen* (or close).** Rendered in a single indented italic block, two or three lines, dry. The line acknowledges, in System-voice, what Theron learned in the case — that his training had filtered out a type of suspect and that he now knows the shape. The line is the chapter's earned LitRPG payoff.
- **Sable seeing Theron's face while he reads the screen.** The reversal of the reveal contract: the camera, for one scene, is on Theron-as-read-by-Sable. Held entirely in action and dialogue. The shift in her registers concretely in what she does, never in what she thinks.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **No Sable interiority.** Not a sentence. Not a fragment. Not a clause that filters the camera into her. Action and dialogue only. The reversal of the reveal contract is rendered by **what she does and says when she watches him**, never by the narrator stepping into her head.
- **No "she realised" / "her feelings tipped" / "she knew now" rendered in prose.** Those are the formula's structural descriptors for the writer, not the chapter's language. The chapter must not contain any sentence that names what is happening for her.
- **No level-up theatrics.** The flavour line is one indented italic block, dry, no fanfare. No stat increase listed. No "+1 to Insight." No allocation prompt. Two or three lines maximum.
- **No second System intervention.** This chapter has one System line and only one. Any additional ping dilutes the load-bearing moment.
- **No Theron lecturing himself about what the flavour line means.** Half a sentence of free indirect reaction is the most allowed. The rest sits in the silence of him reading it twice.
- **No naming of the cottage, the inheritance address, Sable's onward travel, Joren's home region, the niece, the family, the book-two thread.** All of those belong to Ch 38–40. The chapter is in Sannholt, at a harbour-side inn, eating a meal at mid-to-late afternoon.
- **No "they did it together" beat.** No mutual congratulation. No mutual relief. They are tired; the food is in front of them; they eat.
- **No held look. No "their eyes met." No breath-catching. No physical proximity beat.** The romance moves forward because of a shared moral observation; not because of contact, not because of attraction-language. If you find yourself writing a held look — stop.
- **No thematic gloss in the close.** No "and he understood now what the System had meant." No "and the case was finished in the only sense that mattered." End on a concrete physical detail and one small working line between them.
- **No reference to Halsa.** The room behind them is the room behind them; Halsa is in Ottren's inner room; this is not Halsa's chapter. Do not return to her even in passing thought.
- **No foreshadowing of Ch 38–40.** The chapter closes the case in the only sense Step 12 closes the case for this beat. Anything ahead is for the next chapter to find.
