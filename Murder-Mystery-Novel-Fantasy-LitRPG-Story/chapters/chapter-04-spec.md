This chapter is the **first chapter of three** covering Step 2 (Ch 4–6). It carries the discovery, the initial examination, and the System quest ping. Suspect-pool consolidation and the first set of interviews are reserved for Ch 5–6.

## Scenes to hit

1. **Morning, knocking unanswered.** Calden's first round of the day reaches "Vannic's" cabin. The door doesn't open. The corridor neighbours start to notice. Theron — already up, working through his second coffee in his own cabin — registers the quality of Calden's third knock and comes out.

2. **The opening of the cabin.** Calden uses the steward's master key. The door's interior bolt is engaged — they have to draw it back by hand through the steward's narrow service slot. The room is shut from the inside. Vellaine is in the corridor watching. Sable is two doors down with her hand on her latch, not crossing the threshold. The body is on the bed, fully dressed for the day, no visible wound. The window port is sealed; the deck-hatch above is sealed; the chain-trunk that runs through the wall is intact.

3. **The examination.** Theron — class active, irritation at himself for being almost pleased about it, then sharper than that, because the man is dead — works the body and the room. No magistrate aboard; Calden defers to him because the pin is on his coat. He logs: cool to the touch but not cold, no froth, no contusions, no bottle on the side table, the day's collar already pinned. In an outer pocket of the dressing coat: **a small carved wooden fish, no bigger than a knuckle, the kind a child makes with an inherited knife.** He logs it as *odd personal effect* and moves on without underlining. The System pings the quest. He closes the cabin and tells Calden to keep the corridor clear.

## Continuity

Ch. 3 ends with Theron asleep in his cabin on the first overnight passage, the harness chains settled into their night-rhythm. He spent the afternoon pulling the full first-class manifest from the slate and building working categories on each passenger before catching himself doing it; he then put the slate in the desk drawer as a deliberate exercise in restraint. The amended twelfth booking (now under the name M. Tannin, cabin three, original name under sealed administrative note at Calden's discretion) is on record but he didn't pursue it. He found Wisp in the rigging house at second watch; she explained that Mottled does her best work when she believes she's getting away with something and that Gravel only performs when watched. Dinner was quieter than breakfast; "Vannic" came in late, ate with his back to the room, and left before dessert; Vellaine told Theron nobody holds their pin straight in the third week.

Tone in: cozy holiday at the moment of fracture. Theron's investigator-instinct, which he has spent two days disciplining as restraint, becomes the thing the situation needs. The cozy texture does not vanish — the coffee is still good, Calden is still Calden — but the floor of the scene tilts.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- **Do not foreshadow that the body is not Vannic's.** Theron sees a dead Lord Vannic. The reader sees a dead Lord Vannic. The identity is uncontested in the prose.
- **Do not let any character "almost catch" the swap.** Sable's stillness is grief-adjacent, not suspicion. Vellaine's professional eye is on the room, not on the face.
- **Do not cluster plants.** Only the carved fish lands. The calluses do not get re-described during the examination — Theron registers the hand as a hand, not as the hand he already filed in Ch 1.
- **Do not let the carved fish become a thought.** One beat, one line of interior reaction, then the next item. The reader passes it without stopping.
- **Do not use a HUD-style System box.** The quest update is one indented italic block, dry, in voice. Theron's reaction is half a sentence of free indirect, no more.
- **Do not go cold-case in tone.** This is the first death of Theron's professional life. He works with discipline, but his voice does not retreat into clinical distance. He thinks faster, sharper, slower in the right places. He stays himself.
- **Do not give Sable interiority.** She is in the corridor. Theron sees her hand on the latch, sees her not cross the threshold, sees what she does and doesn't do. No interior thought from her.
- **Do not "discover" the locked-room geometry.** The cabin's geometry — interior bolt, sealed port, deck hatch, chain trunk — has been ambiently established in Ch 1–3 (cabin tour, corridor layout, Wisp's rigging). It is named in this chapter, plainly, as Theron walks it. Fair-play contract: the room is seen before the impossibility is asserted.

## Length and shape

Target: **~2,500 words** (range 1,800–3,500). Three scenes as listed above, with the body of the chapter sitting in scene 3 (the examination). Scene 1 is short; scene 2 is the threshold; scene 3 carries the weight.
