Chapter 8 is the middle of the three Step-3 chapters. The pairing has begun; this chapter shows it in motion. Theron came back from the captain with leave; Sable has been working her end. Now they work in parallel, exchange what each has gathered, and the asymmetry surfaces as a working method rather than as a one-time meal scene. The second kindness lands as a *memory* Sable brings to the conversation — she observed it before the death, and brings it now because she's giving Theron her read of "Vannic" as a person.

## Scenes to hit

1. **Theron returns from the captain with leave to push Calden — and finds Sable already has the first new piece. (Late-afternoon, somewhere quiet.)** Theron's been with Captain Joriss; he has leave conditional on Calden's consent, which the steward will give. He goes to find Sable to coordinate. They meet at the second-class lounge bay window — empty at this hour. He runs the structure of what the captain authorized; she has been thinking about Calden in parallel and has an observation that doesn't quite cleanly fit *into* the structure but reshapes the questions Theron will ask. He listens. He concedes the reshape, slowly. The pair-dynamic in operation: he tries to set the agenda; she edits the agenda before it leaves the table.
2. **Sable gives Theron her read of "Vannic" as a person — the way she gives a person to him on the page.** Not a list. A texture. She talks about him the way she talks about anyone — small, specific, neutral, drawn from observation across the boarding and the meals. Inside that texture, beat by beat, she mentions a moment from the second night: a child two tables over going green, the mother fumbling, "Vannic" without looking up sliding a ginger sweet from his own tray-saucer to the steward's tray with a one-finger gesture and a small word so it could move to the child's table. The mother didn't see who it came from. The child stopped going green within about three minutes. Sable mentions it as one beat in a list of beats. Her line: *that's not how that kind of man moves through the world*. Theron hears it, registers it as *Sable building a profile of the victim's social posture*, asks the next question (about whether the lord's general demeanour was performance or temperament), and moves on. The plant lands and leaves.
3. **They split for the work, and Theron — going down the second-class corridor toward Calden's station with the captain's leave folded in his coat pocket — registers that for the first time today he is walking toward a steward rather than toward a clue.** Small concrete shift. He doesn't articulate it. He just notices Calden as a man Calden's height with Calden's morning behind him, and he is going to ask him a difficult question, and he should arrive at the question by the route a person would, not by the route a manifest would. He arrives at the pantry, finds the door open, raises his hand to knock. Scene ends on the raised hand. The conversation itself belongs to a later chapter.

## Continuity

Ch. 7 ended with Theron leaving Sable in the first-class corridor to go find Captain Joriss. He had asked Sable why she'd walked past the steward's station to bring him the manifest herself the day before; her answer was that she'd been filed in his verify column and she'd wanted him to know she was a person he could ask. He went, mortified in the small useful way, to find the captain. Tone carried in: the partnership is now real, both of them know it, neither is talking about it. He's been with Joriss between chapters; he comes out with a measured grant of leave and a small reshape of how he intends to spend the rest of the day.

## What NOT to do this chapter

- The ginger-sweet plant is mentioned EXACTLY ONCE, beat-level, no reprise inside the chapter. No Theron internal-monologue about why a lord would do that. No Sable extended commentary on it. One beat, one line, move on.
- No "almost catch." Theron does not "have a feeling" about the kindness. Sable does not "almost say more" about it. She says her observation in her ordinary voice and goes on to the next item in the list.
- No foreshadowing the Act 2 reveal. Sable's *not how that kind of man moves through the world* is exactly what it says; it is not also a wink at the reader. The System does not ping on the kindness, the accent, or the question of who "Vannic" really is.
- No Sable interiority. We do not enter her head. We watch her speak and act.
- No romance escalation. They are working partners. The warmth is in competence and small concrete courtesies (a chair turned, a folded note left, the practical sharing of bread again), not in glances.
- No HUD-style System box. Max one or two System interventions, both as indented italic lines, and both earned by Theron *seeing* something he hadn't seen before.
- No second plant. The kindness is the only plant. (Verify §8.)
- No Theron summary at chapter end that reframes the kindness. The end is a man walking toward a steward as a man, raising his hand to knock. That's the whole signal.

## Length and shape

Target: **~2,500 words** (range 1,800–3,500). Three scenes as listed above. Scene 1 and Scene 2 may flow as one continuous conversation broken only by a small staging shift (window bay to the settee, or a refilled cup). Scene 3 is short — half a page at most, all walk and arrival.
